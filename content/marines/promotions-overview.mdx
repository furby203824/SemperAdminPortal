## Start Here If You Are New to Marine Corps Promotions

The Marine Corps operates two parallel promotion systems based on rank. Enlisted promotions are governed by MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> (Volume 2, the Enlisted Promotion Manual). Officer promotions are governed by MCO 1400.31D<Citation source="MCO 1400.31D" />. Two HQMC sections run the program. MMPB-10 manages officer promotions. MMPB-11 manages enlisted promotions. The system advances the best and fully qualified Marines through a competitive process. Junior enlisted promotions (Pvt to Sgt) use the JEPES cutting score system based on objective scores plus command input. SNCO promotions (SSgt and above) use selection boards held annually. Officer promotions use statutory selection boards driven by federal law. Your record is your representation at boards. Every Marine is responsible for the accuracy of their record. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, MCO 1400.31D<Citation source="MCO 1400.31D" />, and MMPB published guidance.

## The Two Governing Manuals

### MCO P1400.32D (Enlisted Promotion Manual)

The Marine Corps Order governing all enlisted promotions. Volume 2, with Change 2. Covers.

- Junior enlisted promotions (Pvt to Sgt) via JEPES cutting scores.
- SNCO selection boards (SSgt and above).
- Meritorious promotions (Combat, Non-combat, MCCAP, SDA).
- Remedial selection (ERSB and junior enlisted auto-remedial).
- Eligibility, time-in-grade, time-in-service requirements.
- Commander responsibilities.
- Promotion delays and revocations.

### MCO 1400.31D (Officer Promotion)

The Marine Corps Order governing officer promotions. Covers.

- Statutory selection board framework.
- Officer eligibility criteria.
- Above zone, in zone, below zone framework.
- Convening message procedures.
- Officer correspondence to board president.

## The Promotion Philosophy

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> and MCO 1400.31D<Citation source="MCO 1400.31D" />, the Marine Corps promotion system is competitive. The system advances Marines who are.

- The best in their grade based on demonstrated performance.
- Fully qualified for the responsibilities of the next grade.

This is NOT an entitlement system. Time in grade and time in service alone do not entitle a Marine to promotion. Selection boards and JEPES competition determine who advances.

### Why "Best and Fully Qualified" Matters

The Commandant uses the promotion system to.

- Identify high-performing leaders.
- Ensure leaders at each grade meet Marine Corps standards.
- Maintain readiness through quality leadership.
- Manage force structure (allocations).

Marines who are not selected at a given board may be selected at a future board if they continue strong performance. A pattern of non-selection may indicate the Marine should consider alternative paths (lateral move, special duty, separation).

## Junior Enlisted vs SNCO vs Officer Promotion

### Junior Enlisted (Pvt through Sgt)

- JEPES cutting score system.
- Monthly promotion cycle.
- Cutting scores published in MCTFS (JEPA screen) by IMOS.
- Marines compete by JEPES score against the published cutoff.
- Largely automatic if score meets cutoff and other requirements (PME, TIG, TIS).
- The Junior Enlisted Promotions and Small-Unit Leader Initiative leaves cover this framework.

### SNCO (SSgt through SgtMaj/MGySgt)

- Annual selection boards (SgtMaj/MGySgt, GySgt, SSgt, Reserve SNCO).
- Boards held in fall, winter, spring, summer respectively.
- Marines compete in their IMOS or OCCFLD.
- Selection based on board deliberations of the Marine's complete record.
- The SNCO Selection Boards leaf covers this framework.

### Officer (W-1 through O-9)

- Statutory selection boards driven by federal law.
- Boards held throughout the year for various ranks.
- Marines compete in zones (above, in, below).
- Selection based on board deliberations of the officer's record.
- The Officer Promotion Boards leaf covers this framework.

## Your Record Is Your Representation

The board does NOT see you. The board sees only your record.

### What the Board Sees

- OMPF (Official Military Personnel File) including FITREPs, awards, schools, etc.
- MBS (Master Brief Sheet) showing the aggregate FITREP profile.
- MCTFS data (PFT, CFT, Rifle, Pistol, PME, MOS, etc.).
- Personal correspondence sent under signed cover letter (destroyed after the board).

### What the Board Does NOT See

- You in person.
- Your reputation among peers.
- Your unit's general assessment of you.
- Material added to OMPF AFTER the board adjournment.

### The Critical Implication

Every Marine is responsible for the accuracy of their record. Missing FITREPs, errors in MCTFS, missing awards or PME entries, and incorrect dates all reduce the board's view of your performance.

The Selection Board Preparation (Records and Materials) leaf covers the audit framework.

## Records as Your Career

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, you should.

- Order and review your record at least one year prior to consideration by a board.
- Conduct a self-audit 90 days prior to the board convening.
- Submit corrections to MMPB-22 well in advance of the board.
- Submit personal correspondence to the board president no later than 10 calendar days before the board convenes.

### Tools to Review Your Record

- O-RMA (OMPF-Records Management Application). Digital review and update requests.
- MOL (Marine Online). Aggregate access to OMPF, MBS, MCTFS data.
- M&RA portal. Access at https://www.manpower.usmc.mil/.

The Selection Board Preparation leaf covers the audit checklist.

## FY26 Promotion Policy Updates (MARADMIN 222/26)

Per MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" /> (14 May 2026), three substantive promotion policy changes plus one cascading cancellation.

### 30-Month Sergeant TIS (Effective 1 October 2026)

Per paragraph 1.a and paragraph 4.a, the standard TIS requirement for promotion to Sergeant drops from 48 months to 30 months. Applies to the total force. The Junior Enlisted Promotions leaf covers the new TIS framework.

### 270-Day Sequenced PME Window for SSgt Selectees (Effective FY26 Board Release)

Per paragraph 1.b and paragraph 4.b, Sergeants selected via Sequenced PME for SSgt have 270 days (not 365) after the release of board results to complete both a qualifying resident and non-resident Sergeant course. Active Component and Active Reserve only. The PME Requirements for Promotion leaf covers the new deadline.

### SSgt Alternate Selection List Discontinued (Effective FY27)

Per paragraph 3.c and paragraph 4.b, the FY27 SSgt Promotion Selection Board will NOT select alternates. Promotion allocations are adjusted based on population shortfall forecasts. Active Component and Active Reserve only. The SNCO Selection Boards leaf covers the transition.

### SULI Cancelled (Effective 1 October 2026)

Per paragraph 3.a.2, the Small-Unit Leader Initiative (SULI) per MARADMIN 225/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 225/23" /> is cancelled. The new 30-month standard TIS supersedes the function SULI served. The Non-Competitive Cpl-to-Sgt Promotions leaf covers the transition.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What governs my promotion?

If you are enlisted, MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Volume 2 (the Enlisted Promotion Manual). If you are an officer, MCO 1400.31D<Citation source="MCO 1400.31D" />.

Who runs the program?

MMPB-10 for officers. MMPB-11 for enlisted. Both at HQMC Manpower and Reserve Affairs.

What is the philosophy of the promotion system?

Best and fully qualified. The Commandant promotes Marines who are the best in their grade and fully qualified for the responsibilities of the next grade. Not entitlement based.

How do junior enlisted promotions differ from SNCO promotions?

Junior enlisted (Pvt to Sgt) use JEPES cutting scores (objective + command input). Largely automatic monthly. SNCO (SSgt and above) use annual selection boards reviewing the Marine's complete record. Different processes.

How do officer promotions differ from enlisted?

Officer promotions are statutory selection boards driven by federal law. Enlisted SNCO promotions are also selection boards but governed by Marine Corps procedure. The processes are similar but the legal framework differs.

What does the board see about me?

OMPF (FITREPs, awards, schools). MBS (Master Brief Sheet). MCTFS data. Personal correspondence with signed cover letter. The board does NOT see you in person.

Whose responsibility is record accuracy?

Yours. Every Marine is responsible for the accuracy of their record. Missing or erroneous information reduces the board's view of your performance.

When should I review my record?

At least one year before any board. Conduct a 90-day pre-board audit. Submit corrections well in advance.

What if I am not selected?

Continue strong performance. Many Marines are selected at subsequent boards. Career counseling available through MMEA-64. The Failure of Selection and Non-Selection leaf covers options.

What is the My Eligibility tool?

A tool on the MMPB-11 website where you can verify whether you are eligible for an upcoming SNCO board and what zone you are competing in.
