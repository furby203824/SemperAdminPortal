## Scope

MCO 4400.201 sets strategic policy, procedures, and responsibilities for managing every category of property under Marine Corps control in compliance with Department of Defense (DoD) directives. The order ships as a base order plus seventeen functional-area volumes. The base order carries overarching policy and the volume listing. Each volume issues and updates independently. The strategic goal is full inventory accountability and visibility across the accountable property systems of record, financial systems of record, and DoD-compliant procurement and requisitioning systems. Applicability is the Marine Corps Total Force, including government-furnished property (GFP) in the possession of contractors.

## Audience

- Deputy Commandant, Installations and Logistics (DC I&L). Administers the order. Monitors compliance. Grants exceptions. Updates volumes by functional area.
- Deputy Commandants and Special Staff. Support property management and accountability. Coordinate timely review of proposed changes.
- Commanders, Marine Corps Forces (MARFORs) and Supporting Establishment. Enforce compliance with the policies and procedures. Coordinate timely review of proposed changes.
- Commanding officers and officers in charge. Accept the supply account inside the 60-day window from assumption. Review the outgoing Supply Officer endorsement report.
- Supply officers, accountable officers, and unit responsible officers. Apply Volume 1 accountability and responsibility rules.
- Supply Officer (S-4) staff and warehouse personnel. Run the day-to-day supply, requisition, and inventory functions across volumes 1 through 11.
- Ammunition handlers and arms room custodians. Apply Volume 7 AA&E controls.
- Class VIII medical materiel managers. Apply Volume 12.
- Property Control Officers, Garrison Property Officers, Garrison Mobile Equipment Officers. Apply Volume 15.
- Financial managers, comptrollers. Apply Volume 16 financial treatment and reporting.
- FLIPL investigating officers and approving authorities. Apply Volume 17 Financial Liability Investigation of Property Loss procedures.
- Contractors holding GFP. Subject to the order through their contract terms.

## Commander action items pulled from the base order

1. Make this order available to every Marine and civilian responsible for accounting, reporting, and stewardship of property.
2. Ensure subordinate compliance with the policies and procedures.
3. Coordinate timely review of proposed changes through the chain of command.
4. Direct supply officers and accountable officers to apply the volume corresponding to their functional area.
5. Confirm GFP held by contractors is captured under the order through contract terms.
6. Request exceptions through DC I&L when a unit posture warrants deviation.
