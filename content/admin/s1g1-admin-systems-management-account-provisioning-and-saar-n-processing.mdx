## Role and responsibility

S-1 admin specialists own account provisioning at the unit level. The S-1 clerk receives the SAAR-N request and validates the requested role against the billet. The unit security manager signs the access validation per role-based access rules. The S-1 chief routes the request to MISSO and tracks activation. The S-1 officer signs by direction on privileged role requests.

Source documents flow from billet assignments, MOS qualification certificates, command guidance on role assignment, and the unit access roster. SAAR-N requests route to MISSO for MCTFS and UD-MIPS access and to RAPIDS for workstation operator access. Outputs include the MISSO activation confirmation, the cyber awareness training certificate, the privileged access agreement when applicable, and the OMPF Field Folder systems tab entry.

MCAAT inspects the SAAR-N packet for security manager signature, training proof, role-billet alignment, and activation audit trail. A clerk operating without a current SAAR-N triggers a finding under MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />. Activate access only after training, not before.

## Trigger events

- Inbound Marine reports for S-1 duty needing system access.
- Billet change moves a clerk to a higher access role.
- New system release requires fresh SAAR-N submission.
- Cyber awareness training certificate expires triggering reactivation.
- MOS conversion brings a Marine into the admin field.
- Reservist activates and requires temporary access on Title 10 orders.
- Audit identifies a clerk operating without a current SAAR-N.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the request. Pull the SAAR-N draft from the inbound clerk or the billet change order. Cross-check EDIPI, billet, and requested role. The S-1 clerk runs receipt.
2. Validate billet, MOS, and grade. Confirm the role aligns with the billet, the MOS qualifies the Marine for the role, and the grade meets any minimum threshold per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" /> Chapter 4.
3. Validate need-to-know. The unit security manager confirms role-based access aligns with the duty per SECNAVINST 5211.5<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5211.5" />. The security manager signs.
4. Confirm cyber awareness training. Verify the Marine holds a current annual cyber awareness training certificate per DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />.
5. Build the SAAR-N packet. Complete DD Form 2875 with all required signatures. Attach the training certificate and Privacy Act acknowledgment.
6. Submit MISSO access request. Route DD Form 2875 to MISSO for MCTFS and UD-MIPS access per MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> enclosure 6.
7. Submit RAPIDS workstation access. Coordinate with the RAPIDS operator for workstation provisioning when the role requires CAC issuance or DEERS write access.
8. Train the clerk. Run system-specific training before activation per NAVMC 3500.3E<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.3E" /> T-R event 0102-GENA-1015 performance step 3.
9. Activate the account. Confirm activation in each system. Test a sample transaction in a training environment when available.
10. Document activation. Cut a Page 11 entry recording system access and training completion per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6.
11. Update the active account roster. Add EDIPI, role, system, activation date, and SAAR-N reference. The S-1 clerk owns the roster.
12. File the certified packet. Place the SAAR-N, training certificate, and activation confirmation in the OMPF Field Folder systems tab.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Master record system requiring SAAR-N for access.
- MOL. Self-service surface tied to MCTFS access.
- UD-MIPS. Diary cutting and certification system requiring SAAR-N.
- RAPIDS. CAC and DEERS workstation requiring separate operator certification.
- OMPF. Personnel file access tied to records billet.
- TFSMS. Structure system tied to manpower billet.
- DEERS. Eligibility system accessed through RAPIDS.

### Forms

- DD Form 2875 SAAR-N. Standard access request form.
- Privileged Access Agreement. Required for privileged certifier roles.
- Cyber Awareness Training Certificate. Annual training proof.
- Active Account Roster. Local register of provisioned access.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting system access and training.

## Common pitfalls

- SAAR-N submitted without security manager signature. MISSO rejects on the first review. Verify all signatures before routing.
- Access activated before cyber awareness training. The clerk operates outside policy. Confirm training before submission per DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />.
- Role mismatch with billet. Clerk overprovisioned for the assigned duty. Verify role-billet alignment per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
- Page 11 entry skipped. The OMPF lands without record of access. Cut the Page 11 within 5 working days of activation per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" />.
- Active account roster not updated. The quarterly review picks up the gap. Update the roster at every activation.

## Decision points

- Standard versus privileged access. The 0111 needs MCTFS or UD-MIPS certifier access. Route through the Privileged Certifier Account Authorization workflow with the privileged access agreement attached per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
- Temporary access for activated reservist. The reservist activates for a 90-day window. The S-1 chief routes a time-limited SAAR-N with explicit sunset date. MISSO closes the account at sunset.
- Inbound clerk arrives without prior cyber awareness certificate. The clerk runs cyber awareness training as part of the inbound brief before SAAR-N submission per DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />.
