## Role and responsibility

The Reporting Senior (RS) prepares the FITREP in MOL within the 30-day submission window after the end of the reporting period per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2. The Reviewing Officer (RO) reviews and digitally signs through MOL. The Marine Reported On (MRO) acknowledges through MOL self-service. The S-1 chief tracks the report through MOL submission to MMSB acceptance. The PES clerk operates MOL daily and processes rejections.

Source documents flow from the reporting occasion trigger, the RS draft narrative for Sections A through L, the chain digital signatures, the MMSB acceptance response, and any rejection codes. MOL FITREP actions land in MOL through MMSB to OMPF. Outputs include the certified MOL submission, the MMSB acceptance, the OMPF posting, and the unit PES tracking register.

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2 (Submission Schedule), normal reports submit within 30 days of the end of the reporting period. Adverse reports submit within 60 days per the same paragraph and Chapter 5 paragraph 5 (Timing of Reporting Adversity). Per Chapter 7 (Handling and Submission of Completed Reports), the digital workflow runs through MOL to MMSB.

## Trigger events

- Reporting occasion identified triggering MOL report creation.
- RS opens report in MOL within the first 30 days of the reporting period per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2.
- Temporary duty status reaches 31 days requiring FD report per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3.
- 90-day observation threshold reached per Chapter 3.
- RO change triggers CH report timing per Chapter 3.
- MMSB rejection received requiring correction.
- OMPF posting required after acceptance.

## Processing workflow

1. Validate MOL access. The S-1 chief confirms RS, RO, and MRO each hold current MOL access. Per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />, access requires SAAR-N.
2. Open the report in MOL. The RS opens within the first 30 days of the reporting period per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2 (RS expectations set within the first 30 days).
3. RS prepares Sections D through L. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 4 paragraphs 8 through 16, draft each section per the order.
4. RS digital signature. The RS signs in MOL and submits to RO queue.
5. RO reviews and signs. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 4, the RO reviews. RO digital signature submits to MRO.
6. MRO acknowledges. The MRO views in MOL self-service and acknowledges. Per Chapter 5 paragraph 9, the MRO is permitted to submit a rebuttal for adverse reports.
7. Submit to MMSB. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2, normal reports submit within 30 days of the end of the reporting period. Adverse reports submit within 60 days per Chapter 5 paragraph 5.
8. Track MMSB rejection codes. Pull rejection messages and route corrections. Common rejection reasons include missing data fields, occasion code mismatch, observation period gap, missing signatures.
9. Verify OMPF posting. After MMSB acceptance, the report posts to OMPF. Confirm via MOL Self-Service or PES portal.
10. Update MCTFS PES status flags. Confirm the FITREP status code reflects in MCTFS per MCTFSPRIUM.

## Timeline milestones

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the operational timeline runs as follows.

- Reporting period start. RS sets expectations with MRO within the first 30 days per Chapter 4 references.
- 90-day minimum observation. Per Chapter 3 paragraph 5, observation reports require 90-day minimum unless an exception applies.
- 31 days temporary duty. Per Chapter 3, temporary duty lasting 31 days or longer requires an FD report.
- 89-day rule. Per Chapter 3, exception to policy applies for periods of 89 days or less. RSs shall not write reports under specific circumstances at this threshold.
- Reporting period end. Day 0 of submission window.
- Day 30 submission deadline. Normal report deadline at HQMC per Chapter 3 paragraph 2 (Ensure completed fitness reports arrive at HQMC within 30 days of the end of the reporting period).
- Day 60 submission deadline. Adverse report deadline at HQMC per Chapter 3 paragraph 2 and Chapter 5 paragraph 5.
- MMSB processing window. MMSB Performance Evaluation Branch adjudicates per its suspense calendar (refresh the calendar at MMSB website or PES Smart Card).
- OMPF posting. Following MMSB acceptance, the report posts to OMPF.

## Common pitfalls

- MOL access lapses for the RS or RO before submission. The chain stalls. Validate access per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" /> before reporting occasion.
- Submission past 30 days. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2, normal reports arrive at HQMC within 30 days. Late submission triggers tracking impact.
- Adverse report submitted past 60 days. Per Chapter 5 paragraph 5, adverse timing is strict. Track on a tickler.
- MRO does not acknowledge. The report stalls. Brief the MRO at preparation start on the acknowledgment requirement.
- Rejection codes ignored. The report sits without resubmission. Pull MMSB rejection messages on a fixed cycle and route corrections.
- Digital signature workflow broken by personnel change. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 10 (Special Situations), refresh the chain at every personnel turnover.

## Decision points

- Normal versus adverse submission timing. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3 paragraph 2, normal reports run on 30-day window. Adverse reports run on 60-day window per Chapter 5 paragraph 5.
- Rejection correction versus resubmission. Per Chapter 7, MMSB returns reports for specific corrections. Route per the rejection memo.
- 89-day versus 90-day observation. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 3, the 89-day rule limits report writing under specific circumstances. The S-1 officer validates per the order.
