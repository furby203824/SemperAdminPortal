## Role and responsibility

S-1 admin specialists own system account management for every personnel and pay system the section touches. The S-1 chief supervises account provisioning and quarterly review. The S-1 officer signs by direction on access requests and approves privileged certifier accounts. The unit security manager reviews access against role and need-to-know per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.

Source documents flow from billet assignments, SAAR-N requests, and MOL workflow approvals. Account state lives across MCTFS, MOL, UD-MIPS, RAPIDS, OMPF, TFSMS, and DEERS. Outputs include the active account roster, the privileged certifier list, the quarterly review signature, and the disabled account log.

MCAAT inspects account rosters for stale accounts, unverified certifier credentials, and missing quarterly review signatures. A separated Marine with an active account triggers a finding under DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />. Disable accounts at the moment of detach, not at the next review.

## Trigger events

- Inbound Marine reports for S-1 duty needing system access.
- Outbound Marine detaches and requires account closure within 24 hours.
- Quarterly account review window opens.
- Billet change moving an 0111 to a higher access role.
- System outage requiring backup processing capability.
- New release of a system requiring training before access.
- MISSO support case opened for a system error.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the access request. Pull the SAAR-N or MOL workflow request. Cross-check billet, MOS, and grade against the requested role. The S-1 clerk runs receipt.
2. Validate role and need-to-know. Confirm the access role aligns with the billet and need per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" /> Chapter 4. The security manager signs the validation.
3. Process the account request. Submit to MISSO for MCTFS and UD-MIPS access. Submit to RAPIDS for the issuing-system workstation. The S-1 chief routes.
4. Train the inbound clerk. Run the system-specific training before activating access. Document the training in a Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" />.
5. Activate the account. Confirm activation in each system. Test transaction submission against a test member or training environment.
6. Inventory active accounts. Maintain the unit account roster with name, EDIPI, role, system, and effective date. The S-1 clerk owns the roster.
7. Run quarterly account reviews. Compare the account roster against the unit billet roster. Flag stale accounts. The security manager and S-1 chief sign the review.
8. Disable accounts at detach. Submit account close requests to MISSO and RAPIDS within 24 hours of certified detach per DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />.
9. Audit privileged certifier accounts each month. Verify the certifier list against the diary certification log. The S-1 chief signs the audit.
10. Manage system outages. Coordinate with MISSO on outage status. Run backup processing per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> outage procedures.
11. Document system change requests. Route enhancement and bug requests through MISSO. Track resolution in the unit system log.
12. Close the access cycle. File the SAAR-N, training records, and account close request in the OMPF Field Folder under the systems tab.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. The personnel and pay master record system.
- MOL. The Marine self-service and order delivery surface.
- UD-MIPS. The unit diary cutting and certification system.
- RAPIDS. CAC issuing and DEERS update station.
- OMPF. The official military personnel file system.
- TFSMS. The T-O and T-E manning system.
- DEERS. The benefit and dependency eligibility system.

### Forms

- DD Form 2875 SAAR-N. Standard access request for Navy and Marine Corps systems.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting system training.
- DD Form 2842 DoD CAC PIN Reset Standard Form. Used at RAPIDS for CAC operations.
- Privileged Access Agreement. Required for MCTFS certifier accounts.

## Common pitfalls

- Stale account on a separated Marine. The Marine retains MCTFS access past detach. MCAAT writes the finding under DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />. Disable accounts within 24 hours of certified detach.
- Privileged certifier account held by an untrained Marine. The Marine signs diary lines without the privileged access agreement. Suspend the account and run training before reactivation per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
- Quarterly review skipped. The roster diverges from the billet picture. Run the review on a fixed cycle and sign the roster.
- Outage processing not staged. The system goes down and the section halts. Maintain a backup processing kit with paper templates per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> outage section.
- SAAR-N submitted without security manager signature. MISSO rejects the request. Verify all signatures before routing.

## Decision points

- Privileged certifier authorization. The 0111 reaches the SSgt-with-DoR threshold for certifier access. The S-1 officer signs the privileged access agreement before MISSO activates the role per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
- Outage backup activation. The system outage exceeds the daily processing window. The S-1 chief activates paper backup processing and notifies MISSO.
- Account suspension on misconduct. The Marine is under investigation affecting integrity of the certified record. The S-1 officer suspends the account pending resolution per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" />.

## Authority

This category sits under MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> MCAP enclosure 6, Responsibilities of the Manpower Information Systems Support Office, and MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" /> Marine Corps Cybersecurity Program. The processing workflow maps to NAVMC 3500.3E T-R event 0102-GENA-1015 Manage Marine Online (MOL) modules and supervisory events under 0170-PERA-2009 Manage personnel data analytics. The MCAAT admin track admin-systems-management is the audit reference for this functional area.
