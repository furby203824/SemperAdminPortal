## Start Here If You Are New to SNCO Selection Boards

The Marine Corps holds four SNCO selection boards annually. SgtMaj/MGySgt, GySgt, SSgt, and Reserve SNCO. Each board operates in three phases. Pre-board (data collection, eligibility validation, case folder preparation). Board (case briefs, Discrepancy Notices, IMOS/OCCFLD voting). Post-board (verification, MARADMIN release). Board composition is one Colonel as president, 20 voting members, and 3 recorders. Marines compete by IMOS or OCCFLD in three zones (above, in, below). Up to 10 percent or 2 selections (whichever is greater) come from the below zone at the president's discretion. The Alternate Selection List may be created when full allocations are unfilled. The selection MARADMIN releases within 48 hours after board adjournment. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 3.

This page walks you through SNCO selection boards from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The annual board schedule (four boards per year)
- Board composition (president, voting members, recorders)
- The three phases (pre-board, board, post-board)
- Discrepancy Notices (DNs)
- IMOS and OCCFLD voting
- Above zone, in zone, below zone framework
- The 10-percent or 2-selection below-zone limit
- The Alternate Selection List framework (SSgt list discontinued effective FY27 per MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" />)
- What boards see vs do not see

## Annual Board Schedule

The Marine Corps holds FOUR SNCO selection boards annually. The general schedule (varies by FY).

| Board | Typical Duration | Typical Convene Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SgtMaj and MGySgt | 8 weeks | October |
| Reserve SNCO | 7 weeks | January |
| GySgt | 8 weeks | January or April |
| SSgt | 9 weeks | April or July |

The exact dates are published in the annual board schedule MARADMIN.

### Board Convening Dates Are Not Guaranteed

Per MMPB-11, although convening dates are usually consistent year to year, there are no guarantees. Marines should not assume a board will convene at the same time as the prior year.

## Board Composition

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 3 and MCBul 5420.

### Membership

- 1 Colonel as President.
- 20 other voting board members.
- 21 voting members total (including the president).
- 3 recorders (officer or enlisted) for administrative support.

### Cross-Section Representation

Board members reflect a true cross-section of the Marine Corps with representation from aviation, combat service support, and combat arms occupational fields.

### Member Selection

The Officer Assignment Section (MMOA-3) solicits board membership per the guidance in MCBul 5420. The Director, Personnel Management Division approves the membership.

### What the Enlisted Promotion Section Does

MMPB-11 manages the eligible population, instructs, guides, and provides administrative support to the selection board. MMPB-11 personnel do NOT sit as members of SNCO selection boards.

## Three Phases

### Phase 1. Pre-Board

The Enlisted Promotion Section coordinates with other agencies collecting data for the board. Approximately 60 days before the board (active duty) or 90 days (reserve).

#### Data Collected

- Zones and allocations from MPP-20 (active) or RAP (reserve).
- Adverse information from MIF (NJPs, courts-martial, BCP assignments).
- Separation/retirement status from MMSR.
- Career denial codes (RE-30) from MMEA.
- Nonresident PME completion from MCI.
- Warrant officer/MECEP non-competitive enrollment from MCRC.

#### Case Folders Prepared

Each eligible Marine has a folder containing.

- Fitness reports not in OMPF.
- Photograph (if received) - Note. Photos no longer required for SNCO boards as of October 2021.
- Update material the Marine forwarded under signed cover letter.

#### Convening MARADMIN Released

Approximately 60 days (active) or 90 days (reserve) before the board, MMPB-11 publishes the convening MARADMIN with initial zones and allocations.

#### Refined Zones and Allocations

Approximately 30 days (active) or 45 days (reserve) before the board, MPP-20 or RAP provides refined data. MMPB-11 publishes Change 1 to the convening MARADMIN.

#### Precept Signed

The Director, Personnel Management Division signs the precept (the Commandant's rules of engagement for the board) before the convening date.

### Phase 2. Board

The board convenes (typically Wednesday).

#### Briefs and Training

Recorders arrive 2 days before convening. Board members arrive on the convening date. Members receive briefings from.

- Head, Promotion Branch.
- Director, Personnel Management Division.
- Sergeant Major of the Marine Corps.
- Personnel Sergeant Major of the Marine Corps.

Members are trained on the digitized boardroom application and selection board procedures.

#### Case Distribution

The computer randomly distributes case files (above zone and in zone) among the board members. The president receives all below-zone cases.

#### Case Brief Preparation

Each board member reviews the OMPF of assigned Marines and prepares detailed briefs.

#### Discrepancy Notices (DNs)

During the prep phase, board members may need additional information to brief a case. The briefer submits a DN.

##### DN Examples

- Missing fitness reports.
- Clarification of date gaps.
- Certification of photo info.
- Verification of PME completion.

##### DN Process

- The board recorder researches the request using MCI, MMSB, MCTFS, and other sources.
- Fitness report DNs are submitted to MMSB. 72-hour response time.
- After 72 hours, the recorder informs the briefer of completed DNs. The briefer can require continued pursuit of uncompleted DNs.

##### Incomplete Cases

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, if the board determines a Marine's case is incomplete but sufficient information is available to make a valid recommendation, the Marine will be considered for selection. Marines in this situation are NOT entitled to remedial consideration based on missing material updated after the board.

#### Voting

Once all cases are briefed, the board votes.

##### Voting Procedure

- Vote is by IMOS for active duty USMC and Active Reserve, by OCCFLD for SMCR and IRR.
- Only the number of allocations for each IMOS or OCCFLD may be selected.
- Voting is anonymous. Each member submits "yes" or "no" for each Marine.
- Eligible Marines with the highest "yes" vote count for the assigned allocations in each IMOS or OCCFLD are selected.

##### Below Zone Selections

Below zone Marines DO have a chance for selection. Up to 10 percent of the allocation or 2 selections (whichever is greater) may be selected from below zone at the president's discretion.

### Phase 3. Post-Board

The board verifies selects and non-selects. MMPB-11 publishes the official board report and prepares the executive summary.

#### Outcall Scheduled

An outcall is scheduled with the Deputy Chief of Staff, Manpower and Reserve Affairs / Director, Personnel Management Division.

#### MARADMIN Prepared

The select MARADMIN is prepared. Once the outcall is completed, the board report and select MARADMIN are forwarded to the Director, Personnel Management Division for signature and release.

#### MARADMIN Release Timeline

The selection MARADMIN is normally released within 48 hours after the board has adjourned.

#### Confidentiality

Members of the Promotion Branch are NOT authorized to release names of selects or non-selects. The select MARADMIN is NOT posted to the Promotion Branch website until 48 hours after release.

## Zones

### Above Zone

Marines who were eligible for selection in prior years but were not selected. Above zone Marines receive FULL consideration alongside in-zone Marines. There is NO minimum or maximum allowable number that may be selected from above zone.

### In Zone (Promotion Zone)

Marines who are first-time eligible for selection. The promotion zone may be either extended or reduced during the board. Review the select MARADMIN for zone and allocation changes.

### Below Zone

Marines who would be eligible the following year. The below zone is normally an estimate of the next year's promotion zone. Up to 10 percent of allocations or 2 selections (whichever is greater) may be selected from below zone at the president's discretion.

#### Accelerated Fitness Reports

An accelerated fitness report for Sergeants and Staff Sergeants places a Marine in the below zone population if the Marine is not already in one of the three eligibility zones. Identification depends on compliance with reporting procedures, timely submission to MMPB-23, and the existence of a below zone for that IMOS.

## Alternate Selection List

To minimize shortfalls in the force, an alternate list MAY be created for a board when the full allocation for a PMOS cohort is fulfilled.

### SSgt Alternate List DISCONTINUED Effective FY27 (MARADMIN 222/26)

Per MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" /> paragraph 3.c (14 May 2026), the Staff Sergeant Promotion Board Alternate Selection List is DISCONTINUED effective Fiscal Year 2027 (FY27). The FY27 SSgt Promotion Selection Board will NOT select alternates.

#### What Replaces the Alternate List

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 2.c, the alternate list is replaced by an adjusted annual promotion allocation model. Promotion allocations are adjusted based on population shortfall forecasts. The change streamlines the promotion process and enhances the Marine Corps' ability to produce the required number of Staff Sergeants.

#### Application

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 4.b, the elimination of alternate selects applies only to the Active Component and Active Reserve. The change does not apply to SMCR.

#### Other SNCO Boards

The Alternate Selection List framework remains in effect for the GySgt, MSgt/1stSgt, and SgtMaj/MGySgt boards. MARADMIN 222/26 only addresses the SSgt board.

#### Through the FY26 SSgt Board

The Alternate Selection List framework below remains in effect through the FY26 SSgt Promotion Selection Board cycle.

### Per MARADMIN Paragraph 6 (Selection MARADMINs)

#### 6.a. Draw Case Code AS

The board selects alternates. MMPB-11 reports the draw case code "AS" for all alternate selects.

#### 6.b. Notification

MMPB-11 notifies alternates of their selection via.

- Marine Online (MOL) message.
- Alphabetized list of alternates posted on the Enlisted Promotions Unit website.

#### 6.c. No Failure of Selection

Alternates do NOT incur a failure of selection. A letter annotating selection as an alternate posts to the Marine's OMPF.

#### 6.d. No Seniority Number Until Activation

Marines selected as alternates will NOT have a seniority number reported in MCTFS. Once an alternate is activated, MMPB-11 assigns seniority numbers based on service requirements.

If activated, alternates promote AFTER the primary select list clears.

#### 6.e. OBS Within 30 Days of Activation

Alternates must gain OBS within 30 days of activation notification. The Obligated Service (OBS) and Reenlistment for Promotion leaf covers OBS framework.

#### 6.f. PCA or PCS Implications

Alternates may receive Permanent Change of Assignment (PCA) or Permanent Change of Station (PCS) orders based on service requirements. All assignment policies remain in effect related to OBS and PCA/PCS orders.

### When Alternates Are Activated

Per MMPB-11, alternates are activated when shortfalls in the primary select list emerge (e.g., primary selects who refuse appointment, separate, retire, or otherwise are removed from the list).

### What Activation Means

When activated, the alternate becomes a primary select. MCTFS reflects the seniority number. The alternate is bound by the same OBS, PCA/PCS, and other requirements as primary selects.

## What Boards See vs Do Not See

### Board Sees

- OMPF (FITREPs, awards, schools, etc.).
- MCTFS data (rifle, pistol, PFT, awards, schools).
- Case folder material (FITREPs not in OMPF, photo if received pre-2021, update material from Marine).
- Adverse material (regardless of how minor).
- Below zone records (selectable up to 10 percent or 2 selections).

### Board Does NOT See

- Duty status codes.
- Strength category codes.
- Draw case codes.
- Pending legal or administrative actions.

### Boards Review the Entire Record

The board reviews and considers a Marine's entire military record from private to current rank.

## CY26 Special Program and Slating Boards (MARADMIN 224/26)

Per MARADMIN 224/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 224/26" /> (14 May 2026), the Marine Corps Manpower Management Division announced the CY 2026 USMC enlisted board and panel schedule.

### Scope

These boards and panels are NOT promotion selection boards. They slate or assign Marines to FY27 and FY28 requirements for special programs, B-billet billets, fellowships, and accession opportunities. Promotion to SSgt, GySgt, MSgt/1stSgt, and SgtMaj/MGySgt remains governed by the annual promotion boards covered elsewhere in this leaf.

### Application

Per MARADMIN 224/26 paragraph 5, the schedule applies to the Active Component. Convening dates may shift slightly. Individual MARADMINs publish prior to each board and panel with specific eligibility and coordinating instructions.

### Personal Responsibilities

Per MARADMIN 224/26 paragraph 3, each Marine is personally responsible for ensuring the accuracy and completeness of their Official Military Personnel File (OMPF) and Master Brief Sheet (MBS) before the date the board or panel convenes.

- Use the OMPF-Record Management Application (O-RMA) for the most expeditious OMPF and MBS review.
- Refer to MCO P1070.12K (IRAM) to determine what documents are appropriate for inclusion in the OMPF.
- Verify any OMPF or MBS additions or deletions within the 12-month window.
- Per MARADMIN 582/25, boards and panels evaluate OMPF photographs. Maintain a current photograph in the OMPF.

The Selection Board Preparation leaf covers the OMPF and MBS audit framework in detail.

### Obligated Service Requirement

Per MARADMIN 224/26 paragraph 4, each of these opportunities requires the Marine to meet the associated obligated service (OBS) requirement. Marines who do not currently possess sufficient retainability submit a reenlistment or extension request through the Total Force Retention System (TFRS) under the current retention campaigns. The submission and approval occur prior to execution.

The Obligated Service (OBS) and Reenlistment for Promotion leaf covers the OBS framework.

### Solicitation MARADMINs

Per MARADMIN 224/26 paragraph 2, solicitation MARADMINs publish for each board and panel respectively with specific eligibility requirements and coordinating instructions. Each MARADMIN has specific deadlines and requirements and is adhered to accordingly. Marines targeting a panel review the panel-specific MARADMIN for details.

## Things to Remember

### Communicate Through the Right Channels

You compete by IMOS or OCCFLD. Material is submitted through.

- Personal correspondence to the board president (signed cover letter, 10-day deadline).
- MMPB-11 for general updates.
- MMPB-22 (Records) for OMPF corrections.
- MMPB-23 (PES) for fitness report corrections.

### Material Sent to Board Is Destroyed After

Personal correspondence sent to the board is destroyed upon board adjournment. It does NOT become part of the OMPF. The Selection Board Preparation (Records and Materials) leaf covers this.

### Photos Not Required Since October 2021

SNCO selection boards no longer receive paper or digital photos.

### Confidentiality of Reasons for Non-Selection

The Enlisted Promotion Section does NOT know why a Marine is not selected. A reason for non-selection is NOT noted beside an individual's name. Non-selection reflects the majority opinion of board members that a Marine's record was not as competitive as the records of those selected.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

When are SNCO selection boards held?

Four annually. SgtMaj/MGySgt (October typically), Reserve SNCO (January typically), GySgt (January or April), SSgt (April or July). Exact dates in the annual board schedule MARADMIN.

How is the board composed?

1 Colonel as President. 20 voting members. 3 recorders. Cross-section of aviation, combat service support, and combat arms.

What are the three phases?

Pre-board (data collection, 60-90 days before convening). Board (case briefs, voting, several weeks). Post-board (verification, MARADMIN release within 48 hours of adjournment).

What is a Discrepancy Notice (DN)?

A board member's request for clarification or additional information. Examples include missing fitness reports, date gap clarifications, PME verification. 72-hour response time.

What if my case is incomplete?

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, if sufficient information is available to make a valid recommendation, you will be considered for selection. You are NOT entitled to remedial consideration based on missing material updated after the board.

What zones are there?

Above zone (eligible in prior years, not selected). In zone (first-time eligible). Below zone (next year's estimate, selectable up to 10 percent or 2 selections at president's discretion).

How does below zone selection work?

Up to 10 percent of allocations or 2 selections, whichever is greater, may be selected from below zone at the board president's discretion.

What is an accelerated fitness report?

A FITREP for a Sergeant or Staff Sergeant placing the Marine in below zone if not already in one of the three zones. Depends on compliance with reporting procedures, timely submission, and existence of below zone for the IMOS.

What is the Alternate Selection List?

A list of Marines who are alternates to the primary select list. Created when full allocations for a PMOS cohort are fulfilled. Activated when shortfalls in primary list emerge.

Is the SSgt Alternate Selection List still used in FY27?

No. Per MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" /> paragraph 3.c, the SSgt Alternate Selection List is discontinued effective FY27. The FY27 SSgt Promotion Selection Board will NOT select alternates. Promotion allocations are adjusted based on population shortfall forecasts. The change applies only to the Active Component and Active Reserve.

Are GySgt, MSgt, and SgtMaj boards affected by the FY27 alternate list change?

No. MARADMIN 222/26 only addresses the SSgt board. The Alternate Selection List framework remains in effect for GySgt, MSgt/1stSgt, and SgtMaj/MGySgt boards.

What boards and panels are on the CY26 schedule beyond promotion boards?

Per MARADMIN 224/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 224/26" />, twelve CY26 panels handle special programs and slating to FY27 and FY28 requirements. Congressional Fellowship, FBI National Academy, Service Academies Slating, MCRC Enlisted to Chief Warrant Officer, MGySgt High Visibility Billet, Staff-NCO Degree Completion, Blue Angels, NIU Reserve Masters, SNCO Fellowship, Equal Opportunity Advisor, Marine Attaché, and Personnel Exchange Program Ground Panel. See the CY26 Special Program and Slating Boards section above for the full schedule. Individual solicitation MARADMINs publish per panel.

Are the CY26 special program panels promotion boards?

No. Per MARADMIN 224/26, these panels slate or assign Marines to special programs and B-billet billets. Promotion to SSgt and above remains governed by the annual promotion boards covered elsewhere in this leaf.

Do CY26 special program panels apply to the Reserve Component?

Per MARADMIN 224/26 paragraph 5, the schedule applies to the Active Component only.

How am I notified if I am an alternate?

MOL message and alphabetized list posted on the Enlisted Promotions Unit website. A letter annotating alternate selection posts to your OMPF.

Do alternates incur a failure of selection?

No. Per Selection MARADMIN paragraph 6.c, alternates do NOT incur a failure of selection.

When does an alternate promote?

When activated. Alternates promote AFTER the primary select list clears. Once activated, alternates must gain OBS within 30 days of activation notification.

When are board results released?

Within 48 hours of board adjournment. The Promotion Branch website posts the select MARADMIN 48 hours after release.

Why was I not selected?

The Enlisted Promotion Section does not know specific reasons. The board majority determined other Marines' records were more competitive. Coordinate with MMEA-64 (Enlisted Career Counseling) for guidance on improving competitiveness.
