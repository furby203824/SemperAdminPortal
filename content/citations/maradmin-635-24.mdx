## Scope

MARADMIN 635/24 sets the guiding principles for the ethical use of artificial intelligence (AI) by the Communication Strategy and Operations (COMMSTRAT) community. The MARADMIN supersedes MARADMIN 602/24 (dated 2024-12-17) with broader wording on the scope of permitted AI use. Principles include national security safeguards, transparency about AI-enhanced content, human oversight, and acknowledgment of AI limitations.

## Audience

- COMMSTRAT community. Apply the ethical principles to AI use in execution and evaluation of communication plans and strategies.
- Public affairs officers and Marine Corps communicators. Mandatory adherence to the principles.
- Commanders relying on COMMSTRAT products. Verify products follow the principles.

## Guiding principles

1. AI use aligns with the DOD Principles of Information. Released information is truthful and accurate. AI use is authorized to increase editorial efficiency, inform understanding, support legitimate command narratives, and counter misinformation, disinformation, and malinformation.
2. AI use upholds the responsibility to protect information. Adherence to security, accuracy, privacy, and propriety standards. Only DoD-approved AI technologies are authorized.
3. Imagery and video are uniquely truthful and compelling mediums for informing understanding. AI is barred from creating photo-realistic imagery, video, or news stories for public dissemination. AI is authorized for reviewing written products or for corrective techniques applied to visual information per the DoD Visual Information Style Guide. Products adjusted with AI annotate the adjustment in caption and metadata (example, basic color correction done with AI).
4. AI use is authorized to automate processes and enhance understanding of the information environment.
5. COMMSTRAT protects the trust of key publics and the integrity and legitimacy of commands and missions through the content released. Over reliance on AI risks atrophy of creative and technical skills and challenges proficiency in conventional content creation methods.
