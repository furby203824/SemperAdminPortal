## Start Here If You Are New to Junior Enlisted Promotions

Junior enlisted promotions cover Pvt through Sgt. The system is JEPES-based. Marines earn objective scores from training (PFT, CFT, Rifle, MCMAP, PME, Self-Education) and command input (Character, MOS or Mission Accomplishment, Leadership). Monthly cutting scores by IMOS are published in MCTFS through the JEPA screen. Marines whose JEPES score meets or exceeds the cutoff for their IMOS are selected for promotion if they meet all other requirements (PME complete, TIG, TIS). PFC and LCpl are largely automatic if you meet TIG/TIS and PME. Cpl and Sgt require strong JEPES scores AND PME. Sgt also requires reenlistment commitment and the standard TIS minimum. Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 1.a, effective 1 October 2026, the Sergeant TIS minimum drops from 48 months to 30 months (total force) and the Small-Unit Leader Initiative (SULI) per MARADMIN 225/23 is cancelled. School honor graduates rate meritorious promotion under the 5-percent rule. Late training entries trigger automatic remedial promotion per MARADMIN 559/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 559/23" />. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" />, and MARADMIN 559/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 559/23" />.

## TIG and TIS Minimum Requirements

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> and MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" />, the minimum time in grade (TIG) and time in service (TIS) requirements for junior enlisted promotions.

### Regular Promotion (USMC and USMCR) - Effective 1 October 2026

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 1.a and paragraph 4.a, the Sergeant TIS minimum is 30 months. The change applies to the total force.

| Promotion to | TIG | TIS |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sgt | 12 months | 30 months |
| Cpl | 12 months | 12 months |
| LCpl | 8 months | 9 months |
| PFC | 6 months | 6 months |

### Regular Promotion (USMC and USMCR) - Through 30 September 2026

The legacy TIS framework remains in effect for the transition period.

| Promotion to | TIG | TIS |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sgt (with reenlistment) | 12 months | 3 years |
| Sgt (without reenlistment) | 12 months | 4 years |
| Cpl | 12 months | 12 months |
| LCpl | 8 months | 9 months |
| PFC | 6 months | 6 months |

### Meritorious Promotion (USMC and USMCR)

| Promotion to | TIG | TIS |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sgt | N/A | 18 months |
| Cpl | N/A | 6 months |
| LCpl | N/A | N/A |
| PFC | N/A | N/A |

The meritorious TIS framework for Sgt (18 months) is not modified by MARADMIN 222/26.

### Why TIG and TIS Matter

These minimums are required for promotion eligibility. Marines who do not meet TIG and TIS are not eligible for promotion that month, regardless of JEPES score.

## Sergeant TIS Change (MARADMIN 222/26)

Per MARADMIN 222/26 (14 May 2026), the standard TIS requirement for promotion to Sergeant drops from 48 months to 30 months effective 1 October 2026. The change applies to the total force per paragraph 4.a.

### Why the Change

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 2.a, the FY20 increase from 24 to 48 months under MARADMIN 612/19 favored longevity over performance. The new 30-month TIS strengthens competition among high-performing Corporals and ensures Marines are promoted based on merit and performance.

### What Stays the Same

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 3.a.3, all other eligibility requirements for promotion to Sergeant remain unchanged.

- 12 months TIG.
- JEPES cutting score requirement.
- PME flag "Y" requirement.
- Reenlistment (OBS) requirement per the Obligated Service (OBS) and Reenlistment for Promotion leaf.
- Commander discretion.
- All other existing policies.

### SULI Cancellation

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 3.a.2, the Small-Unit Leader Initiative (SULI) per MARADMIN 225/23 is cancelled effective 1 October 2026. The new 30-month standard TIS supersedes the function SULI served. The Non-Competitive Cpl-to-Sgt Promotions leaf covers the transition.

### What This Replaces

The 30-month TIS replaces the 48-month TIS standard set by MARADMIN 612/19.

## The JEPES Cutting Score System

### What a Cutting Score Is

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, a cutting score is the JEPES score threshold for promotion in a specific IMOS for a specific month. Marines whose JEPES score meets or exceeds the cutoff for their IMOS are selected for promotion if otherwise eligible.

### Where Cutting Scores Are Published

In MCTFS through the JEPA screen.

#### How to Access JEPA

1. Log in to MCTFS.
2. Type "JEPA" from the main menu.
3. Select the desired option.
4. Use F5/F6 to toggle active/drilling reserve/active reserve.
5. Use F7/F8 to scroll up/down.
6. Use F10/F11 to navigate previous/next month.

### Cutting Score of 9999

A cutting score of "9999" indicates the specified MOS to that rank is closed. No promotions to that rank in that MOS for that month.

### When Cutting Scores Release

Per MMPB-11, cutting scores are normally released no later than the 25th of each month. After the mid-month update and extract.

### MCTFS vs MOL Dashboard

The MCTFS JEPES score is the AUTHORITY for promotion purposes. The MOL JEPES Marine Reported On Dashboard may show a different score because.

- MOL Dashboard reflects the current state including data entered after the command cutoff.
- MCTFS calculates the score using data prior to the command cutoff for each promotion period.

For promotion, the MCTFS score is what counts.

### The Command Cutoff Date

The command cutoff is the deadline for data to count toward a given month's promotion. For active duty, changes are made to the promotion parameters and cutoff scores during the third week of each month. For reserve Marines, changes occur during the end of the second month of each promotion quarter.

## How JEPES Score Is Computed

Per MCO 1616.1<Citation source="MCO 1616.1" />, JEPES has four pillars equally weighted at 25 percent each.

- Warfighting (25 percent). Rifle (12.5%) + MCMAP (12.5%).
- Physical Toughness (25 percent). PFT (12.5%) + CFT (12.5%).
- Mental Agility (25 percent). MOS Courses (50% of pillar), MARINENET (20%), Degrees (10%), Self-Education in Grade (10%), Self-Education in Service (10%).
- Command Input (25 percent). Character (8.3% of total), MOS or Mission Accomplishment (8.3%), Leadership (8.3%).

The JEPES (Junior Enlisted Performance Evaluation System) leaf in the Performance Evaluation parent group covers the four-pillar framework in detail.

## How a Marine Is Selected for Promotion

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> and MMPB-11.

1. Marine accumulates JEPES score components throughout the period.
2. Command Input Marks are entered by the Reporting Official.
3. Command cutoff date arrives.
4. MCTFS computes the JEPES score using all data prior to cutoff.
5. MMPB-11 publishes cutting scores by IMOS for the month.
6. Marines whose JEPES score equals or exceeds the cutoff AND who meet other requirements (PME complete, TIG, TIS) are selected.
7. Select grades post on the unit's Diary Feedback Report (DFR).
8. The promotion MARADMIN authorizes the unit to effect the promotion.
9. The IPAC promotes the Marine on the 1st of the month.

## PME Requirements

Per MARADMIN 474/21<Citation source="MARADMIN 474/21" /> and MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />.

### PME Flag

The PME flag in MCTFS must be "Y" for the Marine to be selected for promotion.

### What "PME Complete" Means

The Marine has completed the required PME for the next grade.

- For LCpl. Required PME for Cpl.
- For Cpl. Required PME for Sgt.
- For Sgt promotion to SSgt. Required PME for SSgt (different framework, covered in PME Requirements for Promotion leaf).

### PME Incomplete Marines

Marines who meet the cutting score but are not PME complete will NOT be selected for promotion. The PME flag must be "Y."

### Late PME Entries

If PME is completed but not entered in MCTFS by the cutoff, the Marine may be eligible for junior remedial promotion (covered below).

## School Honor Graduate Meritorious Promotion (5 Percent Rule)

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, school honor graduates may receive meritorious promotion.

### The 5 Percent Rule

The number of meritorious promotions from a class is based on 5 percent of the class size.

### Who Is Eligible

- The 1st place graduate (honor graduate) typically rates promotion if they are eligible for the next grade.
- The 2nd place graduate rates promotion ONLY if the class size is 40 or more (5 percent = 2 promotions).

### Calculating the Number of Promotions

- Class of 40 or more. 5 percent = 2 or more promotions. 1st and 2nd place rate consideration.
- Class of 30. 5 percent = 1.5. Round DOWN. 1 promotion. Only 1st place rates consideration.
- Class of 20. 5 percent = 1.0. 1 promotion. Only 1st place rates consideration.

### Why Round Down

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />, you cannot carry the decimal point or round up. Always round down.

### Eligibility for Honor Graduate Promotion

The Marine must.

- Be the appropriate grade to receive the meritorious promotion.
- Meet TIS minimum.
- Meet other meritorious promotion requirements per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" />.

If 1st place is already a higher grade than the meritorious promotion authorized, the slot may pass to 2nd place, and so on.

## Junior Remedial Promotion Process

Per MARADMIN 559/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 559/23" />, the junior enlisted remedial promotion process is largely automatic in MCTFS.

### How It Works

1. Late entry of training data or command input marks occurs (after command cutoff).
2. MCTFS automatically recomputes the JEPES score.
3. MCTFS screens whether the new score meets a previous month's cutoff.
4. If yes and the Marine is otherwise eligible, MCTFS automatically promotes the Marine in the next promotion cycle with the remedial date of rank.
5. A message is sent to the unit's JEPES module in MOL.

### What Triggers Auto-Remedial

Late entry of training data or command input marks that retroactively brings the Marine's score above a past month's cutoff.

### When Formal Request Is Required (MARADMIN 559/23)

Most junior enlisted remedial promotions are automatic. Formal request from the commanding officer via naval correspondence is only required for.

- Correcting entry-level grades associated with a contracted PFC.
- Non-competitive IMOS promotions.
- Marine Corps Embassy Security Group PME waivers.
- Musician Enlistment Option Program promotions.
- Reservists who changed components after the requested remedial promotion date.
- Correcting a previous DOR for a Marine already promoted.

### IPAC's Role

The unit IPAC can run remedial selection entries in UD/MIPS with TTC 287-003 between the 1st of the promotion month and the day before select grades are run for the following promotion month.

## The Monthly Promotion Cycle

### Active Duty

- Mid-month. Update and extract in MCTFS.
- Third week. Manpower Plans and Policy provides cutting scores.
- 25th of month. Cutting scores released.
- 1st of next month. Promotions effective.

### Reserve

- End of 2nd month of each promotion quarter. Reserve cutting scores released.
- 1st of next month. Promotions effective.

## Things to Remember

### Cutting Scores Change Monthly

The cutting score for your IMOS may change month to month based on actual vacancies and the eligible population. Past cutting scores are not predictors of future cutting scores.

### Below Cutoff but Close

Marines with JEPES scores close to but below the cutoff should focus on improving the score (re-running events, completing courses, earning qualifications) for the next month.

### MCTFS Is the Authority

The MCTFS JEPES score (computed using data prior to command cutoff) is the authority for promotion. Not the MOL Dashboard.

### Re-Running Events

Marines can re-run the PFT or CFT to improve their score. Re-running rifle qualification can also improve. The Mental Agility pillar can be improved by completing more MOS courses, MARINENET courses, or self-education.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What is JEPES?

Junior Enlisted Performance Evaluation System. Replaces the legacy Pro/Con marks for E-1 through E-4. Four pillars equally weighted (Warfighting, Physical Toughness, Mental Agility, Command Input).

What is a cutting score?

The JEPES score threshold for promotion in a specific IMOS for a specific month. Marines whose score meets or exceeds the cutoff are selected if otherwise eligible.

How do I see my JEPES score?

Through the MOL Marine Reported On Dashboard. The dashboard shows your current score and peer comparison.

How do I see the cutting score for my IMOS?

Through MCTFS. Type "JEPA" in MCTFS and select the desired option.

Why is my MCTFS score different from my MOL Dashboard score?

The MOL Dashboard reflects the current state. The MCTFS score for promotion uses data prior to the command cutoff. The MCTFS score is the authority for promotion.

What does "9999" cutting score mean?

The MOS to that rank is closed. No promotions in that MOS that month.

When are cutting scores released?

Active duty. By the 25th of each month. Reserve. End of the 2nd month of each promotion quarter.

What if I meet the cutting score but my PME flag is "N"?

You will NOT be selected for promotion. The PME flag must be "Y." Complete the required PME and have it entered in MCTFS.

Can I be meritoriously promoted as a school honor graduate?

Yes, under the 5-percent rule. 1st place rates consideration. 2nd place rates if class size is 40 or more.

What is junior remedial promotion?

Per MARADMIN 559/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 559/23" />, automatic remedial promotion in MCTFS when late training or PME entries retroactively bring your JEPES score above a past month's cutoff. MCTFS automatically promotes you in the next promotion cycle.

When is a formal remedial request required?

Per MARADMIN 559/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 559/23" />, only for specific situations (entry-level grade corrections, non-competitive IMOS, MCESG PME waivers, MEOP, component changes, DOR corrections).

What happens if I refuse promotion?

A Marine cannot be forced to accept promotion if it requires an extension or reenlistment they are not willing to make. However, refusal of promotion to gain OBS may make the Marine ineligible for future promotions in the same component. Coordinate with the chain of command.

How does TIG/TIS apply?

TIG (time in grade) and TIS (time in service) minimums must be met. Without TIG/TIS, you are ineligible for promotion regardless of JEPES score.

What happens at promotion to Sgt?

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> and MARADMIN 222/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 222/26" />, Sgt promotion requires the standard TIS minimum (30 months effective 1 October 2026 per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 1.a) and the reenlistment (OBS) requirement per the Obligated Service (OBS) and Reenlistment for Promotion leaf. The OBS requirement is unchanged per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 3.a.3.

When does the 30-month Sergeant TIS take effect?

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 3.a, the 30-month TIS takes effect 1 October 2026. The change applies to the total force (active component, active reserve, and SMCR).

Is SULI still in effect?

Per MARADMIN 222/26 paragraph 3.a.2, SULI is cancelled effective 1 October 2026. The new 30-month standard TIS supersedes the function SULI served. The Non-Competitive Cpl-to-Sgt Promotions leaf covers the transition.
