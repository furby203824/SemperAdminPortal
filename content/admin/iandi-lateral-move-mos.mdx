## What Lateral Move Is

Per MCO 1300.51<Citation source="MCO 1300.51" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 2, lateral move is the process by which Marines change their primary MOS. Lateral moves support force structure needs by reallocating personnel from over-strength MOSs to critical and undermanned MOSs.

For Reserve component members, lateral moves have unique considerations because of unit-specific staffing.

## Why Lateral Moves Matter

The Marine Corps adjusts its force structure based on operational needs. Lateral moves serve.

### Force Balancing

- Members in over-strength MOSs might have limited career opportunities
- Critical MOSs need additional manpower
- Lateral moves redistribute personnel

### Career Opportunity

- Members might pursue MOSs with better career prospects
- Promotion opportunities differ by MOS
- Specific bonuses or programs might incentivize moves

### Operational Requirements

- New mission requirements drive demand for specific MOSs
- Force restructuring requires personnel reallocation
- Critical operational needs prioritize lateral moves

## Eligibility for Reserve Component Lateral Moves

Per MCRAMM, Reserve member lateral moves require.

- Active SMCR drilling status
- Sufficient time remaining in current obligation
- Meeting standards for the new MOS (testing, prerequisites)
- Available billet in the new MOS at qualifying location
- Command endorsement

Specific eligibility per current MARADMIN.

## Two Lateral Move Categories

### Career Progression MOS (CPM)

Lateral moves into MOSs designated for career progression.

- Critical MOSs needing manpower
- Specific bonuses might apply
- Active SMCR retention required
- Commitment to additional service

### Standard Lateral Move

Lateral moves outside CPM designation.

- Member-initiated MOS change
- Specific qualification requirements
- Might or might not have incentive

## Performance Steps for Lateral Move Processing

### Step 1 - Verify Eligibility

When member requests lateral move.

- Verify SMCR drilling status
- Verify sufficient remaining obligation
- Verify member meets new MOS prerequisites
- Verify available billet in new MOS

### Step 2 - Submit Application

Member submits formal application.

- Specific request package
- Member's current qualifications
- Justification for the move
- Command endorsement

### Step 3 - Coordinate with HQMC

The application routes through HQMC for approval.

- HQMC Manpower review
- MOS Sponsor coordination
- Decision on application

### Step 4 - Schedule Training

If approved.

- Member attends required school for new MOS
- IADT or specific training periods
- Specific training duration based on MOS

### Step 5 - Update MOS in MCTFS

After training completion.

- New MOS becomes primary
- Previous MOS might continue as additional
- UD/MIPS transaction updating MOS data

### Step 6 - Coordinate Unit Reassignment

If unit reassignment is needed.

- Member transfers to unit with new MOS billet
- Drilling location might change
- New unit administrative processing

## Drilling Requirements During Move

A Marine in process of lateral move continues normal drilling.

- Continues drilling with current unit during application
- Schools and training might modify drilling temporarily
- Returns to drilling at new MOS unit after training

## Pay Implications

Lateral moves might affect pay.

### CPM Bonuses

- Specific bonuses for designated CPMs
- Paid as part of incentive structure
- Coordinated with HQMC

### Special Duty Assignment Pay

- Some lateral moves into SDA-designated MOSs
- SDAP starts when member reports to SDA billet

### Reserve Bonuses

- Specific reserve component bonuses for lateral moves
- Per current SRIP MARADMIN

## Common Lateral Move Errors

These hit during processing.

1. **Application without prerequisites.** Member applies without meeting new MOS standards. Counsel and verify.
2. **No available billet.** Member approved without billet at qualifying location. Coordinate billet before approval.
3. **MOS update missed.** Training completed but MCTFS still shows old MOS. Process UD/MIPS update.
4. **Bonus eligibility not verified.** Member assumes bonus but does not qualify. Verify against SRIP.

## Lateral Move Documentation

For lateral move actions, admin needs.

- Application package
- Command endorsement
- HQMC approval
- Training completion documentation
- New unit assignment documentation

## Coordination Touchpoints

Lateral move processing touches.

- **HQMC Manpower**: Application processing
- **MOS Sponsor**: New MOS standards verification
- **Member's current unit**: For drilling continuity
- **Member's gaining unit**: For new MOS billet
- **Training School**: For required training

## Related Roles

Lateral moves affect Reserve members pursuing career changes.

- **Marines** apply for lateral move and complete training.
- **Leaders** counsel members and process applications.
- **Commanders** endorse applications and support member career development.
