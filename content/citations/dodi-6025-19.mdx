## Scope

DODI 6025.19 establishes the Individual Medical Readiness (IMR) Program. The instruction implements Sections 1074a, 10149, and 10206 of Title 10 USC. The DoD sets a Total Force Medical Readiness (TFMR) goal of 90 percent or higher for each Military Service and DoD Component. Partially Medically Ready (PMR) goal is 15 percent or lower for the Active Component and 25 percent or lower for the Reserve Component. Signed 13 July 2022 by Gilbert R. Cisneros, Jr., USD(P&R). Change 1 signed 21 January 2026 by Sean O'Keefe.

## IMR Categories and Elements

- Section 3.1 IMR Categories. Fully Medically Ready (FMR), Partially Medically Ready (PMR), Not Medically Ready (NMR), Medical Readiness Indeterminate (MRI).
- Section 3.2 IMR Elements. Six elements drive category determination.
- Section 3.3 DoD Periodic Health Assessment (PHA).
- Section 3.4 Deployment Limiting Medical Condition (DLMC) Status.
- Section 3.5 Dental Readiness.
- Section 3.6 Immunization Status.
- Section 3.7 Medical Equipment.
- Section 3.8 Laboratory Studies (HIV, blood type, DNA).

## Total Force goals

- TFMR. 90 percent or higher across all Services.
- PMR Active Component. 15 percent or lower.
- PMR Reserve Component. 25 percent or lower.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- 10 USC 1074a, 10149, 10206. Statutory IMR authority.
- MCRAMM Chapter 6 (MCO 1001R.1L). Reserve medical standards application.
- BUMEDINST 6320.65 series. Navy Medicine policy on IMR.
- MCO 5100.29C Volume 9. Marine Corps Safety Program coverage of medical readiness.
- NAVMC 3500.105 series. T&R Manual events tied to IMR currency.
