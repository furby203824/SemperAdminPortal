## Start Here If You Are New to the Childcare Provider Travel Pilot

Per MARADMIN 573/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 573/24" /> paragraph 4, the Childcare Provider Travel Pilot reimburses Marines for the cost of bringing a designated childcare provider to the new PDS when on-base Child Development Center (CDC) services are unavailable within 30 days of the Marine's report date. Up to $500 for CONUS PCS. Up to $1,500 for OCONUS PCS. The pilot runs from 1 October 2024 to 30 September 2027. Reimbursement covers commercial transportation only. Air, rail, bus, transportation to and from the terminal, en route lodging, or rental car at departure. The pilot supports Marine families through PCS transitions where the local CDC has wait-list constraints.

## What the Pilot Covers

Per MARADMIN 573/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 573/24" /> paragraph 4, the Childcare Provider Travel Pilot reimburses Marines for the travel cost of a designated childcare provider relocating to the Marine's new PDS during PCS.

The pilot recognizes the operational reality of CDC wait-list constraints at many installations. Families often arrive at a new PDS without immediate access to on-base childcare. Bringing a designated childcare provider from outside the local area bridges the gap.

## Pilot Period

The Childcare Provider Travel Pilot runs from 1 October 2024 through 30 September 2027. Marines on PCS orders during this window are eligible if they meet the criteria. The pilot status is reviewed before the 2027 sunset date for potential extension or transition to permanent policy.

## Eligibility Criteria

Per MARADMIN 573/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 573/24" /> paragraph 4.

### Marine on PCS Orders

The Marine is on official PCS orders to a CONUS or OCONUS PDS.

### CDC Unavailable Within 30 Days

The on-base Child Development Center at the gaining installation has no available slot for the Marine's child within 30 days of the Marine's report date.

### Designated Childcare Provider

The Marine designates a specific childcare provider to travel to the new PDS. The provider is identified in the reimbursement request.

### Documentation of CDC Unavailability

The Marine produces documentation from the gaining installation CDC showing the wait-list status or unavailability. This documentation supports the reimbursement claim.

## Reimbursement Caps

### CONUS PCS

Up to $500 for transportation costs of the designated childcare provider for a CONUS PCS.

### OCONUS PCS

Up to $1,500 for transportation costs of the designated childcare provider for an OCONUS PCS. The higher cap reflects the cost of international travel.

## Authorized Expenses

Per MARADMIN 573/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 573/24" /> paragraph 4, the reimbursement covers commercial transportation only.

### Air, Rail, Bus

- Commercial airfare for the childcare provider.
- Commercial rail or bus fare.

### Transportation To and From Terminal

- Commercial transportation between the provider's home and the departure airport, train station, or bus terminal.
- Commercial transportation between the destination terminal and the new PDS area.

### En Route Lodging

Lodging during the provider's travel to the new PDS when overnight stays are required during transit.

### Departure Rental Car

A rental car at the departure location if the provider's transportation requires a rental car for the initial leg.

### NOT Authorized

The reimbursement does NOT cover.

- Childcare provider compensation or wages.
- Provider housing at the new PDS beyond en route travel lodging.
- Provider meals beyond standard transportation-related expenses.
- Provider visa or immigration costs.
- Provider personal expenses unrelated to travel.

## Documentation Requirements

The Marine submits the reimbursement request through DTS with supporting documentation.

### Required Documentation

- CDC wait-list or unavailability statement from the gaining installation.
- Designation of the childcare provider (name, relationship, role).
- Receipts for all transportation expenses.
- Endorsement on PCS orders if required by the gaining unit.

### Receipts

All claimed expenses require receipts regardless of amount. The pilot is strictly receipt-based.

## DTS Submission

The Marine submits the Childcare Provider Travel reimbursement through the Defense Travel System (DTS) on DD Form 1351-2<Citation source="DD Form 1351-2" /> (Travel Voucher).

### Workflow

1. Marine receives PCS orders to a new PDS.
2. Marine contacts the gaining installation CDC for wait-list status.
3. CDC issues wait-list documentation showing 30-day or greater unavailability.
4. Marine designates a childcare provider.
5. Marine arranges commercial transportation for the provider.
6. Marine retains all receipts.
7. After arrival at the new PDS, Marine submits the voucher in DTS with receipts and CDC documentation.
8. AO approves and routes to DFAS for payment.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

How much do I get reimbursed?

Up to $500 for CONUS or $1,500 for OCONUS. The cap covers total commercial transportation costs.

How long is the pilot?

1 October 2024 through 30 September 2027.

What does "CDC unavailable within 30 days" mean?

The on-base Child Development Center at the gaining installation has no available slot for your child within 30 days of your report date. The CDC issues a wait-list or unavailability statement.

Who qualifies as a designated childcare provider?

The Marine identifies the provider in the reimbursement request. Common designations include family members or trusted childcare professionals traveling to support the family during the PCS transition.

Does the reimbursement cover the provider's wages?

No. The reimbursement covers commercial transportation only. Provider compensation, housing at the new PDS, and other living expenses are at the Marine's expense.

What transportation modes qualify?

Commercial air, rail, or bus. Transportation to and from the terminal. En route lodging if travel requires overnight stays. Departure rental car if needed for the initial leg.

How do I document CDC unavailability?

Contact the gaining installation CDC directly. Request the wait-list status or unavailability statement. Attach the documentation to your DTS voucher.

What if my OCONUS provider travel exceeds $1,500?

The cap is $1,500 for OCONUS. Costs above $1,500 are at the Marine's expense.
