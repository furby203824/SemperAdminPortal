## Scope

MARADMIN 612/19 announced authority for early reenlistment, delegated reenlistment approval authority, and changed promotion requirements policy. The MARADMIN increased the Time in Service (TIS) requirement for promotion to Sergeant from 24 months to 48 months. The Sergeant TIS requirement was reduced back to 30 months under MARADMIN 222/26 effective 2026-10-01. Applicability is the Active Component.

## Audience

- Marines pursuing reenlistment. Apply the early reenlistment framework.
- Career planners. Process reenlistments under the delegated authority.
- HQMC MMEA. Adjudicates eligibility.
- Corporals targeting promotion to Sergeant. The TIS requirement increased to 48 months under this MARADMIN.

## Key changes

- Early reenlistment authority granted.
- Reenlistment approval authority delegated to certain commanders.
- Sergeant TIS requirement increased from 24 months to 48 months.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- MCO P1400.32D. Marine Corps Promotion Manual, Volume 2, Enlisted Promotions.
- MCO 1040.31. Enlisted Retention and Career Development Program.
- MARADMIN 222/26. Forthcoming Changes to TIS for Promotion to Sergeant. Reduces TIS back to 30 months effective 2026-10-01.
- MARADMIN 225/23. Establishment of the Small-Unit Leader Initiative (SULI). Created as a workaround to the 48-month TIS. Cancelled by MARADMIN 222/26.

## Status

Released 2019-10. Originator CMC Washington DC MRA MM. Applicability is the Active Component. The 48-month Sergeant TIS rule from this MARADMIN is superseded by MARADMIN 222/26 effective 2026-10-01 (returns to 30 months).
