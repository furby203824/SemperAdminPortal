## Scope

MARADMIN 463/23 announces the Self-Initiated Referral Process for Mental Health Evaluations (MHE) of Marines under the Brandon Act (Section 704 of Public Law 117-81). The MARADMIN implements DTM 23-005 effective 2023-05-05. Marines initiate a referral process for an MHE through their commanding officer (CO) or supervisor in the grade of E-6 or above. The Brandon Act aims to reduce stigma and foster a help-seeking culture. Applicability is Active-Duty Marines and members of the Active Reserve.

## Audience

- Marines on Active Duty. Request a referral for an MHE at any time, for any reason, in any environment.
- Commanding officers and supervisors in the grade of E-6 or above. Receive the referral request and route to a mental health provider as soon as practicable.
- Reserve Component commands. Establish referral plans as soon as practicable for Marines not on Active Duty.
- OSCAR providers. Embedded mental health providers at commands. Primary source of mental health care.
- Primary Care Managers (PCMs). Issue referrals to civilian healthcare network.
- Marine and Family Programs (MFC). Owns the program.

## Referral process

- Marines request a referral for any reason or basis. No requirement to provide a reason.
- Commanders refer Marines to a mental health provider as soon as practicable.
- Patient rights and confidentiality protected per DTM 23-005.
- Treat MHE referrals like other medical service referrals to the maximum extent possible.
- Command-directed MHE concerns continue under DoDI 6490.04 (concerns about behavior, performance changes, fitness for duty).
