## What the System Recognizes

DON-level policy lives in SECNAV M-1650.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1" />, the Navy and Marine Corps Awards Manual, implementing SECNAVINST 1650.1J<Citation source="SECNAVINST 1650.1J" />. <Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1" /> MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> implements the SECNAV manual for Marines. Both apply in tandem. When the manuals diverge on a procedural detail, MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> is your read for Marine-specific implementation, and SECNAV M-1650.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1" /> is the controlling DON-level standard.

MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> anchors three lanes of recognition. <Citation source="MCO 1650.19J Ch 1" />

- Personal Military Decorations. Individual achievement, valor, sustained superior performance. NAM, NCM, MSM, BSM, and above.
- Unit Awards. Recognition of the unit. PUC, MUC, NUC, MUM, JMUA.
- Service and Campaign Medals. Time in service, campaign participation, foreign awards.

You touch the first two lanes regularly. The third lane mostly fires automatically through service records.

## Leader's Four Jobs

- Spot. Watch the section for sustained or significant achievement worth recognizing.
- Draft. Write the narrative with specific events, dates, and measurable outcomes.
- Route. Move the package through the endorsement chain on time.
- Verify. Confirm the award lands in OMPF after approval.

A Marine deserving an award does not write the package. You do.

## What Counts as a Valor PMD

Per SECNAV M-1650.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1" /> paragraph 1A.2.b, valor decorations are the Medal of Honor, Navy Cross, Silver Star Medal, and any medal awarded with a "V" device.<Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1 1A.2.b" /> Valor packages carry a separate timeline (45 days from act, 10 working days per echelon review, 12 months final action) and priority handling at every level.

The choosing-the-award and routing-and-endorsements leaves carry the operational detail.

## When to Recognize

Three triggers carry obligation.

- End-of-tour. The Marine PCSes. The achievement window closes.
- Sustained superior performance. The Marine has earned it through the cycle.
- Specific act. Heroism, valor, exceptional contribution to a single event.

A fourth trigger sits outside the formal system. A Marine doing strong work who does not warrant a formal award still earns recognition. The recognizing-without-awards leaf covers Letters of Appreciation, Letters of Commendation, and Page 11 commendatory entries.

## What the System Costs the Marine When You Skip a Step

- Skipped spot. The Marine carries the achievement on the section's memory and nowhere else.
- Skipped draft. The Marine never sees the recognition reach the chain.
- Skipped route discipline. The package sits on a desk past the PCS departure.
- Skipped OMPF verification. The award approves but never appears in records. Promotion boards never see it.

## Where Marine Online Sits

The Marine verifies awards on Marine Online. Your section verifies on Marine Online. Pre-board records audits verify on Marine Online. If the award is not in OMPF, the award did not happen as far as the system is concerned.
