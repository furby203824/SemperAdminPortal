## Scope

DoDD 4500.09 establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for DoD transportation and traffic management activities. The directive sets the DoD exemption to General Services Administration (GSA) transportation policy under Section 501 of Title 40 USC. The directive redesignates Commander, US Transportation Command (USTRANSCOM) as the DoD single manager for the DoD Customs and Border Clearance Program. Signed 27 December 2019 by Deputy Secretary of Defense David L. Norquist. Change 2 signed 29 May 2025 by Deputy Secretary of Defense Stephen A. Feinberg. The companion document, DoD 4500.9-R Defense Transportation Regulation (DTR), provides the procedural rules.

## Audience

- USD(A&S). Owns DoD transportation and traffic management policy.
- DoD Component Heads. Implement at component level.
- Commander, USTRANSCOM. Single manager for DoD transportation execution.
- Secretary of the Navy and Marine Corps Logistics Command (MCLC).
- Distribution Process Owners and traffic management offices.
- Personal property shipping offices supporting PCS moves.

## Sections

- Section 1 General Issuance Information. Applicability, policy, Change 2 summary.
- Section 2 Responsibilities. USD(A&S), DoD Component Heads, SECNAV, military department secretaries, CJCS, USTRANSCOM.
- Section 3 DoD Support for Movement of Non-DoD Traffic. Best interest, procedures.
- Glossary. Acronyms and definitions.
- References.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- DoD 4500.9-R. Defense Transportation Regulation (DTR). The procedural implementation document for transportation policy across DoD.
- Joint Travel Regulations (JTR). DTR Part IV governs personal property and household goods rules cross-referenced in JTR Chapter 5.
- 40 USC Section 501. Statutory basis for the DoD GSA exemption.
- MCO 5800.16 Volume 11. Marine Corps Legal Administration Manual covering transportation-related claims.
- Personal Property Consignment Instruction Guide (PPCIG).

Active. Effective 27 December 2019 with Change 2 effective 29 May 2025.
