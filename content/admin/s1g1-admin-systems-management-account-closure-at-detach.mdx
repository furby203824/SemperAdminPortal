## Role and responsibility

S-1 admin specialists own account closure at detach for every system the section touches. The S-1 chief tracks each detach against the active account roster and submits closure requests within 24 hours of certified detach. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the close request and validates the closure against the disabled account log. The unit security manager audits closures during the quarterly account review.

Source documents flow from the certified UD-MIPS detach transaction, the OMPF Field Folder closeout, and the disabled account log. Closure requests route to MISSO for MCTFS and UD-MIPS accounts and to the RAPIDS workstation operator for CAC and DEERS access. Outputs include the closure confirmation per system, the dated entry in the disabled account log, and the audit-trail PDF filed in the OMPF Field Folder systems tab.

MCAAT inspects the disabled account log for closure within 24 hours of certified detach, paper trail completeness, and quarterly review reconciliation. A separated Marine retaining MCTFS access past 24 hours triggers a finding under DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />. Close at certified detach, not at the next batch cycle.

## Trigger events

- Certified UD-MIPS detach transaction posts.
- Marine reports to a gaining unit and is removed from the detach unit roster.
- Voluntary or involuntary separation order executes.
- Retirement order executes with the retirement date in MCTFS.
- Death in service triggers immediate account closure and OMPF freeze.
- Reservist demobilizes back to SELRES, IRR, or Standby.
- Reduction in force separation finalizes.

## Processing workflow

1. Detect the detach event. Pull the certified detach transaction from UD-MIPS at the moment of submission. Cross-check EDIPI, losing RUC, and detach date. The S-1 clerk runs detection.
2. Pull the active account roster for the departing Marine. Identify every system the Marine touches (MCTFS, MOL, UD-MIPS, RAPIDS, OMPF, TFSMS, DEERS). The S-1 clerk pulls.
3. Build the closure request packet. Stage one closure request per system with EDIPI, role, system, and effective close date.
4. Submit MISSO closure requests. Route requests for MCTFS and UD-MIPS access through the MISSO support channel per MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> enclosure 6.
5. Submit RAPIDS closure. Coordinate with the RAPIDS workstation operator for CAC suspension and DEERS access removal at the moment of detach.
6. Remove MOL administrator permissions. Use the MOL administrator interface to remove module-level permissions for the departing Marine per NAVMC 3500.3E<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.3E" /> T-R event 0102-GENA-1015 performance step 5.
7. Confirm closure per system. Pull confirmation messages from MISSO and the RAPIDS log within 24 hours per DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />.
8. Update the disabled account log. Record EDIPI, system, close date, and confirmation reference for every closure. The S-1 clerk owns the log entry.
9. Stage the audit-trail PDF. Print closure confirmations and stage in the OMPF Field Folder under the systems tab per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 3.
10. Reconcile against the next quarterly review. Validate the disabled account log against the unit billet roster at the next quarterly account review cycle.
11. Brief any closure exception. On a delayed closure, missed system, or reactivation request, brief the unit security manager within 24 hours per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
12. Close the cycle. Mark the detach complete in the unit account log with the closure confirmation set attached. The S-1 chief signs the closure entry.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Account state closed at the MISSO request.
- MOL. Module-level permissions removed by the S-1 chief through the MOL administrator interface.
- UD-MIPS. Certifier and clerk roles closed at the MISSO request.
- RAPIDS. CAC suspension and DEERS access removal handled at the workstation.
- OMPF. Read access closed for the departing clerk.
- TFSMS. T-O and T-E access closed for the departing manpower clerk.

### Forms

- MISSO Account Close Request. Standard form for MCTFS and UD-MIPS closure.
- DD Form 2842 RAPIDS Workstation Operator Form. Used for CAC and DEERS access removal.
- Disabled Account Log. Local register tracking each closure with confirmation reference.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting account closure at detach where applicable.

## Common pitfalls

- Closure request batched to end of week. The account stays open past 24 hours. The unit takes a finding under DOD 8500.01E<Citation source="DOD 8500.01E" />. Submit closure at certified detach, not on a batch cycle.
- Only MCTFS closed, RAPIDS access remains. The Marine retains CAC capability and DEERS write access. Confirm closure per system, not only the primary access.
- Closure submitted, confirmation not pulled. The closure status sits assumed without proof. Pull MISSO and RAPIDS confirmations within 24 hours and file with the closure log entry.
- Closure log not reconciled at quarterly review. Stale closures sit unverified across cycles. Reconcile every quarter against the unit billet roster per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />.
- Closure exception not briefed to the security manager. The security manager finds the exception during audit. Brief any delayed or missed closure within 24 hours of detection.
- MOL administrator permissions overlooked. The Marine retains module-level access through MOL even after MCTFS closure. Remove MOL permissions through the administrator interface per NAVMC 3500.3E<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.3E" />.

## Decision points

- Reactivation request post-closure. The departed Marine returns on a new assignment within 90 days. The S-1 officer routes a fresh access request through SAAR-N per MCO 5239.2B<Citation source="MCO 5239.2B" />. Reactivation requires a new access request, not a reopen.
- Privileged certifier closure timing. The departing certifier is the only certifier on shift. The S-1 chief schedules closure at the end of the certifier's last shift, not the start of detach day. Coordinate hand-off with MISSO before submission.
- Account preservation request. The Marine transfers to a sister command on the same installation. The S-1 officer confirms whether the account is RUC-tied (close required) or installation-tied (transferable) per MISSO guidance and MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> enclosure 6.
- Death in service closure. The Marine dies on duty or off duty in active status. The S-1 officer routes immediate closure of all accounts and freezes the OMPF for casualty processing per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 3.
