## What Separation Pay Is

Per 10 USC 1174<Citation source="10 USC 1174" /> and DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 35, separation pay compensates members involuntarily separated from active duty under specific conditions. The pay recognizes the disruption of involuntary separation and supports the transition to civilian life.

Separation pay differs from retirement pay. Retirement pay is for members completing 20+ years. Separation pay is for members separated before retirement under qualifying conditions.

## Categories of Separation Pay

Per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 35.

### Full Separation Pay

For members involuntarily separated and qualified for full separation pay.

- Specific qualifying separation reasons
- Honorable or General (Under Honorable Conditions) characterization
- 6+ years of active duty service typically required
- Calculated as 10% of annual basic pay times years of service

### Half Separation Pay

For members separated under conditions warranting half separation pay.

- Less than fully qualifying conditions
- Some categories of separation
- 50% of full rate

### Reservist Separation Pay

For Reserve members involuntarily separated from drilling status.

- Specific qualifying conditions
- Different calculation per DOD FMR

## Eligibility for Separation Pay

A member receives separation pay when meeting all of these.

- Active duty status at time of separation
- Involuntarily separated (not voluntary discharge)
- 6 or more years of active duty service (or specific reservist conditions)
- Honorable or General characterization
- Not eligible for retirement pay
- Specific separation reason qualifies per DOD FMR

## Disqualifying Conditions

A member does not receive separation pay if.

- Less than 6 years active duty (specific exceptions)
- Voluntary separation
- OTH or Bad Conduct discharge
- Eligible for retirement
- Specific other DOD FMR provisions

## Calculation of Separation Pay

Per 10 USC 1174<Citation source="10 USC 1174" /> and DOD FMR.

### Full Separation Pay Formula

```
Separation Pay = 10% x (Years of Active Service) x (12 x Monthly Basic Pay)
```

Example: Marine with 10 years service and $4500 monthly basic pay.

- Annual basic pay: $4500 x 12 = $54,000
- Separation pay: 10% x 10 x $54,000 = $54,000

### Half Separation Pay Formula

```
Separation Pay = 5% x (Years of Active Service) x (12 x Monthly Basic Pay)
```

Same example with half pay: $27,000.

## Tax Treatment

Per IRS rules.

- Full separation pay: Subject to federal income tax
- Tax withholding at separation
- Specific state tax treatment varies

## Voluntary Separation Programs

Per various DOD initiatives.

### Voluntary Separation Incentive (VSI)

A voluntary separation program with annual installment payments.

- Member volunteers for early separation
- Pay structured as annual payments over multiple years
- Specific qualifying conditions
- Used during force draw-down periods

### Special Separation Benefit (SSB)

A voluntary separation program with lump sum payment.

- Member volunteers for early separation
- Lump sum payment at separation
- Specific qualifying conditions
- Used during force draw-down periods

VSI and SSB programs activate during specific authorization periods. Most current periods do not have these programs active. Verify current MARADMINs.

## Performance Steps for Separation Pay Processing

### Step 1 - Verify Eligibility

When processing member separation.

- Verify involuntary nature of separation
- Verify years of active service
- Verify discharge characterization
- Verify member not eligible for retirement
- Verify specific separation reason qualifies

### Step 2 - Calculate Amount

Apply the separation pay formula.

- Identify member's monthly basic pay at separation
- Calculate annual basic pay
- Multiply by 10% (full) or 5% (half)
- Multiply by years of active service
- Result is separation pay amount

### Step 3 - Process via UD/MIPS

Apply separation pay transaction.

- Specific separation pay transaction code
- Effective date of separation
- Calculated amount
- Federal tax withholding applied

### Step 4 - Verify Pay Action

Confirm processing.

- Pull EDFR
- Verify separation pay calculation accurate
- Coordinate with DFAS for payment processing

### Step 5 - Document Future Repayment Obligation

If member receives separation pay, future re-entry to military service may require repayment.

- Document separation pay receipt
- Member acknowledges potential repayment obligation
- Specific situations require repayment if member rejoins service

## Future Repayment Obligation

Per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 35, members who receive separation pay and later rejoin military service may have to repay separation pay.

- If member returns to active duty before specific period, separation pay must be repaid
- Repayment from future military pay if applicable
- Specific recoupment procedures per DOD FMR

This obligation should be clearly documented at separation.

## Common Separation Pay Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Eligibility not verified.** Member separated voluntarily but separation pay processed as involuntary. Wrong category.
2. **Years of service miscalculated.** Wrong total years used. Verify against MCTFS.
3. **Wrong rate applied.** Full vs Half separation pay confused. Verify per DOD FMR criteria.
4. **Tax withholding incorrect.** Federal tax not properly withheld. Coordinate with DFAS.
5. **Future repayment obligation not documented.** Member unaware of potential repayment if rejoining service.

## Separation Pay vs Retirement Pay

Critical distinction.

| Aspect | Separation Pay | Retirement Pay |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| Eligibility | < 20 years, involuntary separation | 20+ years |
| Form | Lump sum | Monthly annuity |
| Duration | One-time | Lifetime |
| Tax | Federal income tax applies | Tax applies |
| Future obligation | Possible repayment | None |

A member with 20+ years draws retirement pay, not separation pay.

## Separation Pay vs Disability Severance Pay

Disability severance pay is different from separation pay.

- Disability severance: For members separated due to medical disability not qualifying for retirement
- Separation pay: For members separated for other involuntary reasons
- Different calculation methods
- Different eligibility criteria

Disability severance pay is detailed in its own page.

## Voluntary Separation - No Separation Pay

Members who voluntarily separate (resignation, refusal to reenlist) generally do not receive separation pay.

- Voluntary departure does not qualify
- Member receives final pay and any earned but unused leave
- No separation pay benefit

Members considering voluntary separation should understand this before deciding.

## Audit Implications

Separation pay is audit-relevant.

- Eligibility errors create payment to ineligible members
- Calculation errors create over or underpayment
- Repayment obligations not documented create future recovery issues
- Tax withholding errors create year-end reconciliation issues

PAC accuracy on separation pay supports unit audit posture.

## Documentation Required

For separation pay actions, PAC needs.

- Separation orders showing involuntary nature
- Total years active service from MCTFS
- Discharge characterization documentation
- Pay history for monthly basic pay calculation
- Member acknowledgment of repayment obligation

## Quality Control

Before submitting separation pay transactions.

- Verify involuntary separation
- Verify years of active service
- Verify rate (full or half)
- Verify monthly basic pay accurate
- Verify discharge characterization qualifies

## Coordination Touchpoints

Separation pay processing touches.

- **Member's separation processing**: Through MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" />
- **DFAS**: Final pay calculation and processing
- **VA**: For veterans benefits coordination
- **Member's tax preparer**: For tax planning

## Related Roles

Separation pay affects involuntarily separated members.

- **Marines** facing separation receive separation pay if eligible.
- **Leaders** counsel members on separation pay during separation processing.
- **Commanders** ensure proper documentation of separation circumstances.
