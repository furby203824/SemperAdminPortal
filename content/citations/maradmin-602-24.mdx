## Scope

MARADMIN 602/24 sets the guiding principles for the ethical use of artificial intelligence (AI) by the Communication Strategy and Operations (COMMSTRAT) community. The principles balance AI efficiency against national security interests and public trust. Key aspects include safeguarding national security, transparency about AI-enhanced content, human oversight in information release, and acknowledgment of inherent AI limitations. The source page reads CANCELLED. MARADMIN 635/24 supersedes this message.

## Audience

- Communication Strategy and Operations (COMMSTRAT) community. Apply the ethical principles to AI use.
- Public affairs officers and Marine Corps communicators. Mandatory adherence to the principles.
- Commanders relying on COMMSTRAT products. Verify products follow the principles.

## Guiding principles

1. AI use aligns with the DOD Principles of Information. Released information is truthful and accurate. AI is barred from deception, manipulation, and misinformation.
2. AI use upholds the responsibility to protect information. Adherence to security, accuracy, privacy, and propriety standards. Only DoD-approved AI technologies are authorized.
3. Imagery and video are uniquely truthful mediums for conveying actual events. AI is barred from creating photo-realistic imagery, video, or news stories for public dissemination. AI use is limited to reviewing written products or streamlining authorized adjustments to visual information per the DoD Visual Information Style Guide. Products adjusted with AI annotate the adjustment in both caption and metadata (example, basic color correction done with AI).
4. Build and maintain proficiency in conventional content creation methods. Safeguard against over reliance on AI. Avoid atrophy of creative and technical skills.
5. AI use is authorized to automate processes and enhance understanding of the information environment.
