## Role and responsibility

The S-1 officer or adjutant owns constituent inquiry processing at the unit level. The S-1 chief tracks suspense and runs the staffing chain. The S-1 clerk logs inquiries and pulls supporting documentation. The commander signs the substantive response unless authority is delegated by direction. The Marine Corps Office of Legislative Affairs (OLA) holds the master congressional liaison interface at HQMC.

Source documents flow from congressional offices through SECNAV Office of Legislative Affairs (OLA) and HQMC OLA, the constituent letter forwarded by the congressional caseworker, supporting Marine Corps records (with FOIA and Privacy Act screening per foia-release-on-inquiries sub-page), policy citations supporting the response, and the chain of command endorsement. Outputs include the certified response letter, the case packet, the inquiry register entry, and the OMPF Field Folder copy when the inquiry concerns an individual Marine.

The USMC Caseworker Handbook (2013, 113th Congress Edition) is the OLA reference for congressional caseworkers. The handbook covers restrictions on release of information, personnel policies, casualty affairs, and community outreach. Update the handbook reference against the current OLA publication.

## Trigger events

- Congressional office routes a constituent inquiry through SECNAV OLA or HQMC OLA.
- Member of Congress submits an inquiry directly to the unit or to the commander.
- Constituent submits a Privacy Act release authorization with the inquiry.
- White House referral routes a constituent letter through the executive secretary.
- Annual constituent inquiry posture brief cycle with the commander.

## Inquiry routing channels

Constituent inquiries route through three primary channels per the Caseworker Handbook and SECNAVINST 5730.5:

- SECNAV Office of Legislative Affairs (OLA). The DON congressional liaison office. SECNAV OLA routes inquiries to the appropriate DON Component.
- HQMC Office of Legislative Affairs (OLA). The Marine Corps congressional liaison office. HQMC OLA routes inquiries to the appropriate Marine Corps program manager.
- Direct congressional contact. A Member of Congress contacts a unit commander or a Marine Corps program manager directly. The receiving unit notifies HQMC OLA per MCO 5740.4.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the inquiry. Pull the constituent letter and the congressional cover letter. Capture the originator, the subject, the named Marine (if applicable), and the suspense date.
2. Verify the Privacy Act release. For inquiries about an identifiable Marine, verify the Privacy Act release authorization signed by the Marine per 5 USC 552a. Without a release, the response is limited to non-Privacy-Act-protected information.
3. Log the inquiry. Enter in the unit congressional register with date received, originator, subject, action officer, and suspense date.
4. Identify the suspense. Apply the SECNAVINST 5730.5<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5730.5" /> suspense (typically 5 working days for interim, 30 days for substantive) or the suspense set by the originating office.
5. Route to the action officer. Coordinate with the operations officer, legal staff, the manpower officer, or other staff section holding the action knowledge.
6. Coordinate the response. Pull supporting documentation, the Marine's record (with Privacy Act release), and the relevant policy citations. The S-1 chief assembles the response file.
7. Identify the responsible Marine Corps program. The Caseworker Handbook covers the standard program areas (recruiting, reserve, assignments, special pays, promotions, casualty affairs, etc.). Identify the program holder for response authority.
8. Draft the response. Author the response per SECNAVINST 5730.5<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5730.5" /> format and SECNAV M-5216.5 naval letter format with the Marine Corps supplement MCO 5216.20B<Citation source="MCO 5216.20B" />. Lead with the response to the constituent question, follow with supporting facts, close with command position.
9. Run the staffing chop chain. Route through legal, public affairs, and any other affected staff section. Capture chops on a chop sheet.
10. Route for commander signature. The commander signs the substantive response unless authority is delegated by direction per MCO 5740.4<Citation source="MCO 5740.4" />.
11. Submit the response. Route through the same channel the inquiry arrived. Capture confirmation of receipt.
12. File the certified case packet. Place the inquiry, the response, the supporting documentation, the chop sheet, and the Privacy Act release in the unit congressional file.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Congressional register. Local register tracking unit congressional inquiries.
- HQMC OLA portal. Routes congressional inquiries to and from the unit.
- SECNAV OLA portal. DON congressional liaison interface.
- OMPF. Holds individual records pulled for inquiry support (with Privacy Act release).
- Unit records management system. Archives certified case packets.

### Forms

- Naval letter format per SECNAV M-5216.5. Standard response format.
- Memorandum format per SECNAV M-5216.5. Internal staffing format.
- Privacy Act Release Authorization. Marine consent for release of personal records.
- Chop Sheet. Documents the staffing chain.
- DD Form 577 Signature Card. Documents by direction signature authority for routine acknowledgments.

## Common pitfalls

- Privacy Act release missing on Marine-specific inquiry. The response includes Privacy Act-protected information without consent. Verify the release at receipt per 5 USC 552a.
- Suspense tracked from routing date instead of receipt date. The unit submits late. Track from receipt per SECNAVINST 5730.5<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5730.5" />.
- Response drafted without legal chop. The response carries an unverified legal claim. Capture legal chop on every substantive response.
- Commander signature skipped on substantive response. The response goes out under by direction signature. Route substantive responses for commander signature per MCO 5740.4<Citation source="MCO 5740.4" />.
- Caseworker Handbook treated as authoritative. The handbook is a reference guide for caseworkers. Authoritative responses tie to current Marine Corps policy, not the handbook.

## Decision points

- Substantive versus interim response. The action officer needs more time. The S-1 officer routes an interim response within 5 working days and the substantive response within 30 days per SECNAVINST 5730.5<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5730.5" />.
- Privacy Act release route. The inquiry asks about a specific Marine. Verify the Privacy Act release authorization. Without a release, route the response through legal for FOIA Exemption b(6) balancing per DODD 5400.07<Citation source="DODD 5400.07" />.
- Signature authority. The response is routine acknowledgment, substantive response, or commander-only response. The S-1 officer routes per MCO 5740.4<Citation source="MCO 5740.4" />.
- HQMC OLA versus direct response. The unit responds directly to the constituent through the originating congressional office, or routes through HQMC OLA for HQMC-cognizant programs.
