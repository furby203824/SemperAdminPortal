## Scope

MARADMIN 135/26 solicits nominations for the FY26-2 Marine Corps Artificial Intelligence (AI) Fellowship programs. DC I, Service Data Office (SDO), partners with the Naval Postgraduate School (NPS) to send Marines to NPS for a project-based AI fellowship. Cohorts are interdisciplinary teams creating new algorithms, technologies, and solutions tied to mission problems. Applications close 2026-04-30. Selection committee convenes on or about 2026-05-04. Notifications no later than 2026-05-15.

## Audience

- Marines O-1 through O-5. Officer-level applicants.
- Marines E-4 through E-8. Enlisted-level applicants.
- Chief Warrant Officers 1 through 5. CWO applicants.
- Government civilians GS-5 through GS-14. Civilian applicants.
- First O-6 commander in the applicant chain. Signs the command endorsement letter required for the application.
- Naval Postgraduate School (NPS). Holds operational control of fellows during the program.
- Current commands of selected Marines. Retain administrative control. Fund the TAD trips.

## Program structure

- AI Fellows fall under NPS operational control for the duration. Current command retains administrative control. Telework daily.
- Cohort size. No fewer than two Marines per cohort.
- Program length. 5 months.
- Three unit-funded TAD trips to NPS Monterey campus. Approximate cost $5,000.
- TAD 1. 08 to 2026-06-12.
- TAD 2. 24 to 2026-08-27.
- TAD 3. 02 to 2026-11-05.
- Output. Publishable, quality research paper based on the capstone project required for graduation.

## Eligibility

- Unclassified command-sponsored, actionable AI use case with associated data identified.
- Demonstrated experience self-directing in a semi-unstructured environment.
- Remote and telework capable. Personal computer and internet connection required throughout.

## Application process

1. Confirm eligibility at the time of submission. Late or incomplete applications are rejected.
2. Inform the chain of command of the intent to apply.
3. Submit a command endorsement letter signed by the first O-6 commander in the chain through the application portal. Applications missing the endorsement letter are rejected.
4. Apply online at https://forms.osi.apps.mil/r/KuXD4BgLmg.
5. Portal closes at 2359 (EST) on or about 2026-04-30.
6. Indicate program preference (either or both).

## Action items

1. Identify Marines and civilians in the unit who hold an actionable AI use case with associated data.
2. Coordinate the O-6 command endorsement letter.
3. Confirm applicants are remote and telework capable.
4. Schedule unit funding for the three TAD trips (approximately $5,000).
5. Coordinate with NPS for operational control transfer if selected.
