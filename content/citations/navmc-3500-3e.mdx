## Scope

NAVMC 3500.3E establishes training standards, regulations, and policies for Marines in the manpower and administration occupational field. The manual contains the collective and individual training requirements to prepare units to accomplish their combat mission. It is the primary T&R reference for MOS 0102 (Manpower Officer), 0111 (Administrative Specialist), 0147 (Equal Opportunity Advisor), 0149 (Substance Abuse Control Specialist), 0160 (Postal Officer), 0161 (Postal Clerk), 0170 (Personnel Officer), and 0171 (Manpower Information Systems Analyst).

## Structure

The manual contains 11 chapters and 2 appendices. Chapter 1 provides an overview of the T&R program including event coding, combat readiness percentage (CRP) calculation, and event composition. Chapter 2 lists Marine Corps Tasks (MCT) supported by the community. Chapter 3 contains collective events. Chapters 4 through 11 contain individual events specific to each MOS. Appendix A contains acronyms. Appendix B contains terms and definitions.

## Audience

Commanders use this manual to determine pre-deployment training requirements and build unit training plans. Formal schools and training detachments use it to create programs of instruction. S-1 officers, adjutants, admin specialists, and PAC personnel use the event standards and references for day-to-day performance.

## T&R Event Coding

Events use a 4-4-4 alphanumeric system (MOS-FunctionalArea-Level). Individual events range from 1000-level (core skills, formal school) through 2200-level (advanced core plus skills). Collective events range from 3000-level (team/section) through 9000-level (command element). Functional areas include GENA (General Administration), PERA (Personnel Administration), MPMN (Manpower Administration), and OPER (Operational Administration).
