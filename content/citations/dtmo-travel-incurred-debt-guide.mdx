## Scope

The DTMO Guide for Managing Travel-Incurred Debt is the comprehensive Defense Travel System (DTS) reference for identifying, processing, and resolving travel-incurred debts across DoD. Version 2.0, dated 12 August 2016. The guide walks each role through the debt lifecycle from initial recognition through final collection or write-off. The companion short-form reference is the DTMO Understanding a Travel Debt information paper.

## Audience

- Travelers identifying a debt situation.
- Authorizing Officials (AOs) and Reviewing Officials.
- Defense Travel Administrators (DTAs).
- Disbursing officers and Debt Management Offices.
- IPAC travel clerks and S-1 pay clerks.
- Finance officers handling DUE US recoveries.

## Chapters

- Chapter 1. Introduction.
- Chapter 2. Roles in Debt Management. Traveler, AO, Reviewing Official, DTA, Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS), Debt Management Office.
- Chapter 3. Recognizing a Travel Debt.
- Chapter 4. Trip Cancellation After Travel Advance.
- Chapter 5. Trip Cancellation After Scheduled Partial Payment (SPP).
- Chapter 6. Post-Payment Audit Recovery.
- Chapter 7. DUE US Voucher Processing in DTS.
- Chapter 8. Debt Collection Procedures.
- Chapter 9. Salary Offset.
- Chapter 10. Hardship Waivers and Write-Offs.

## Key procedures

- Traveler initial notification on debt recognition.
- Traveler second notification.
- Trip cancelled after non-ATM advance or SPP paid.
- Post-payment recovery workflow.
- Salary offset under 5 USC 5514.
- Voluntary repayment plans.
- Debt write-off thresholds.

## Note on availability

The 2016 v2.0 guide is no longer hosted on the active DTMO site. The current short-form replacement on travel.dod.mil is the Understanding a Travel Debt information paper (June 2025). The full 2016 guide remains in DoD archives and on service-component travel reference sites for historical reference.
