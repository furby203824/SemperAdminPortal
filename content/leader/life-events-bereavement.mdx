## Lead With the Person

A Marine grieving does not need a checklist first.

- Acknowledge the loss.
- Listen.
- Offer specific support, not platitudes.
- Coordinate immediate needs (leave, transportation, communication).
- Refer to chaplain.

The documentation can wait 24 to 48 hours. The conversation cannot.

## Bereavement and Emergency Leave

Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" />, bereavement and emergency leave types apply. <Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" />

- Coordinate immediate leave for travel and family support.
- Submit per unit leave procedures.
- Track the leave to start and return.
- Coordinate billet coverage during the absence.

Some bases support immediate emergency leave with documentation finishing post-departure. Coordinate with S-1.

## When a Dependent Has Died

Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> within 10 working days.

- Certified death certificate from the issuing authority.
- NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> documenting the dependent loss.
- Verification of dependent removal from DEERS.
- BAH adjustment if the rate changes.

The death certificate is certified. Photocopies fail.

## SGLI Beneficiary Considerations

If the deceased was an SGLI beneficiary.

- Brief the Marine on the implications.
- Coach the Marine to review the beneficiary list.
- Coach the Marine to update SGLV-8286 if changing.
- Respect the Marine's timing on the decision.

Pressing the Marine to update SGLI on the day of death is not appropriate. Press in the weeks after as the Marine settles.

## RED-S Update

If the deceased was a designated RED-S contact.

- Brief the Marine.
- Coach the Marine to update on Marine Online.
- Verify the update landed.

## Casualty Assistance Coordination

If a dependent has died, casualty assistance services apply.

- Brief the Marine on Casualty Assistance Calls Officer (CACO) services.
- Coordinate with S-1 on the CACO assignment.
- Support the Marine through the casualty assistance process.

Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" />. <Citation source="MCO 3040.4" />

## When the Death Is of a Marine Family Member Not on Records

A parent, sibling, close friend, or other family member outside the dependent records still produces grief.

- Coordinate emergency leave per the policy.
- Refer to chaplain or MFLC.
- Adjust counseling cadence.
- Watch for performance impact.

The documentation footprint is smaller. The human footprint is the same.

## When the Death Was the Marine's Spouse

This carries the heaviest documentation load.

- All marriage-driven changes reverse.
- Ex-spouse death and separation are different from divorce. The spouse was on records as deceased.
- SGLI beneficiary almost always requires review.
- Children's status may change.
- Family Care Plan if children become the Marine's sole responsibility.

Coordinate with CACO. Coordinate with chaplain. The Marine carries enormous load.

## Watch for the Long Tail

Grief does not close at the leave return.

- Adjust counseling cadence for 90 to 180 days.
- Watch for performance impact, withdrawal, substance use.
- Refer to behavioral health if indicators rise.
- Coordinate with Force Preservation if risk indicators escalate.

A Marine returning from bereavement leave assumed back to normal is a Marine the section is failing.

## Common Bereavement Failures

- Documentation pressed before grief is acknowledged.
- Emergency leave delayed by paperwork.
- SGLI and RED-S left untouched for years.
- Counseling cadence reverts immediately.
- Long-tail grief impact missed.

## Where to Go Next

- Divorce and custody coaching for the parallel personal disruption.
- Family Care Plan oversight if the Marine becomes a sole parent.
- Wellness category for the grief and risk track.
