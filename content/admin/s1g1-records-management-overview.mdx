## Role and responsibility

S-1 admin specialists own records management at the unit level. The records clerk runs daily records handling. The S-1 chief audits records inventory against SSIC structure and NARA dispositions. The S-1 officer signs by direction on records destruction and transfer to the National Archives.

Source documents flow from records creation events, SSIC classification rules, NARA-approved retention schedules per SECNAV M-5210.1, and disposition triggers (end of retention, transfer to NARA, destruction). Records actions touch every staff section. Outputs include the unit records inventory, the destruction certificate, the transfer manifest, and the audit log.

MCAAT and the IGMC Records Management FA inspectors check SSIC accuracy, NARA disposition compliance, records inventory, and Directives Control Point coordination. Per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, every record requires NARA-approved disposition. Statutory authority sits at 44 USC Chapter 31<Citation source="44 USC Chapter 31" />.

## Trigger events

- New record created requiring SSIC classification.
- Records reach end of retention period.
- Annual records inventory cycle opens.
- IGMC Records Management FA inspection notice arrives.
- Records transfer to gaining unit at PCS.
- Records destruction requires destruction certificate.
- Directives Control Point coordination requested.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify records by SSIC. Apply Subject Standard Identification Code per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" /> at record creation.
2. Apply NARA-approved retention. Per SECNAV M-5210.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.1" />, every record carries an NARA-approved retention period.
3. Maintain unit records inventory. The records clerk maintains a per-SSIC inventory.
4. Process records life cycle. Creation, maintenance, use, and disposition per 44 USC Chapter 31<Citation source="44 USC Chapter 31" />.
5. Coordinate with the Directives Control Point. The DCP holds the unit directive index per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />.
6. Run records audits. Audit inventory against SSIC and NARA disposition on a fixed cycle.
7. Process records transfer or destruction. At end of retention, route per the disposition (destroy, transfer to NARA, retain longer).
8. Train clerks. Each clerk handling records receives records management training.
9. Brief records posture. The S-1 officer briefs the commander on inventory health, audit findings, and dispositions completed.
10. Stage records history. Maintain records history for MCAAT and IGMC inspection.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Records management system. Local repository for records inventory.
- Directives Control Point. Index of unit directives.
- NARA portal. Authoritative source for retention schedules.
- OMPF Field Folder. Holds personnel records subject to records rules.

### Forms

- Records Inventory Sheet. Local inventory by SSIC.
- Records Destruction Certificate. Required at destruction per MCO 5210.11F.
- Records Transfer Manifest. Required for transfer to gaining unit or NARA.
- SF 135 Records Transmittal and Receipt. Standard transfer form.

## Common pitfalls

- Records destroyed without disposition authority. Per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, every destruction requires NARA disposition authorization.
- SSIC misclassified at creation. The record sits in wrong category. Train clerks on SSIC at induction per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />.
- Records inventory not maintained. The unit cannot produce records on demand. Maintain inventory per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />.
- Directives Control Point bypassed. The directive index lags. Coordinate with DCP at every directive change.
- Annual records audit skipped. Stale records sit unverified. Audit on a fixed cycle.

## Decision points

- Retention extension versus destruction. The record reaches end of retention but has open litigation hold. Extend retention per NARA disposition rules per SECNAV M-5210.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.1" />.
- Transfer to NARA versus retain locally. Per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, the disposition determines path.
- Electronic versus paper records. Per SECNAV M-5210.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.1" />, electronic records may satisfy retention. The records clerk classifies.

## Authority

This category sits under MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" /> Marine Corps Records Management Program and SECNAV M-5210.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.1" /> Department of the Navy Records Management Manual. Statutory authority sits at 44 USC Chapter 31<Citation source="44 USC Chapter 31" />. S-1 responsibilities sit at MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> MCAP enclosure 4. The processing workflow maps to NAVMC 3500.3E T-R events 0102-GENA-1006 (Supervise Marine Corps records program), 0111-GENA-1002 (Maintain Marine Corps records), 0111-GENA-2008 (Maintain Marine Corps records), and 0111-GENA-2104 (Supervise Marine Corps records management program). The IGMC Records Management FA checklist provides the audit baseline.
